# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits | **Duration:** 3 school weeks | **Cohort:** 120 students in four classes

Each group of three submits one shared practical report. Each student submits an individual analysis and reflection.

## 1. Criterion table

**Band scores:** For 4-mark criteria: Emerging 1, Developing 2, Proficient 3, Exemplary 4. For 3-mark criteria: Emerging 0, Developing 1, Proficient 2, Exemplary 3. Missing evidence may receive 0 and may activate a blocking decision.

| Criterion | Marks | Component | Emerging | Developing | Proficient | Exemplary |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| **GP1: Practical plan and circuit method** | 4 | Group practical | Plan is incomplete; circuit steps and intended observation are unclear. | Workable basic plan identifies a circuit change and observation but has gaps. | Clear, ordered plan identifies what will change, be observed and recorded. | Precise, justified and repeatable plan anticipates comparisons and limitations. |
| **GP2: Results and data representation** | 4 | Group practical | Results are sparse or unclear, with no usable organisation. | Some results are presented in a table or representation, with omissions or errors. | Results are complete, organised and represented suitably so patterns can be seen. | Results are accurate and consistently represented, making relationships and limits clear. |
| **GP3: Conclusion, report quality and contribution record** | 4 | Group practical | Conclusion is absent or unrelated; report or contribution record is incomplete. | Simple conclusion uses limited evidence; report and role record are partly coherent. | Report gives an evidence-based explanation of energy transfer and includes an accurate statement signed by all members. | Explanation is precise and well supported, acknowledges limitations, integrates the report and is consistent with the signed contribution statement. |
| **IA1: Data selection and processing** | 4 | Individual analysis | Data source is unclear or processing is too incomplete to support a claim. | Own or authorised data is identified; basic processing has gaps or errors. | Correctly selects and processes personally collected or teacher-authorised supplied data. | Purposeful, accurate processing compares relevant features and makes patterns clear. |
| **IA2: Energy-transfer reasoning** | 4 | Individual analysis | Describes observations without explaining energy transfer. | Identifies a possible transfer pattern but gives limited reasoning. | Explains energy transfer with logical links to relevant data. | Integrates patterns and contrasting evidence into a precise explanation. |
| **IA3: Evaluation and application** | 4 | Individual analysis | Gives no evaluation or makes an unsupported application. | Names a limitation, improvement or application with little justification. | Evaluates a limitation and proposes a data-linked improvement or application. | Weighs limitations and prioritises a justified improvement, prediction or application. |
| **IR1: Contribution and process reflection** | 3 | Individual reflection | Gives a vague account of role or process. | Describes own role and one effect on group work. | Evaluates contribution and collaboration using specific evidence. | Critically analyses decisions, responsiveness and effect on the outcome. |
| **IR2: Learning and next steps** | 3 | Individual reflection | Learning or a next step is not stated. | States basic learning and a general next step. | Explains changed understanding and identifies a specific next action. | Synthesises learning and justifies a transferable next action. |

## 2. Mark allocation check

| Component | Calculation | Total |
|---|---:|---:|
| Group practical report | GP1 4 + GP2 4 + GP3 4 | **12** |
| Individual analysis task | IA1 4 + IA2 4 + IA3 4 | **12** |
| Individual reflection | IR1 3 + IR2 3 | **6** |
| **Portfolio total** | 12 + 12 + 6 | **30** |

The group result is one shared 12-mark result for the group. Every criterion has a maximum of 4 marks, which is below the 8-mark limit.

## 3. Checkpoint schedule

| Date | Submission | Who checks it | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1/Day 5** | Group practical plan; each student’s individual analysis outline, including intended data source. | Class teacher checks completeness, feasibility and data authorisation. | Missing group plan: GP1 capped at 3/4. Missing individual outline: IA1–IA3 each capped at 3/4. |
| **Week 2/Day 5** | Group draft results; each student’s individual analysis draft. | Class teacher checks results, data source, authorship and progress. | Missing group results draft: GP2 capped at 3/4. Missing analysis draft: IA1–IA3 each capped at 3/4. |
| **Week 3/Day 5** | Full shared report, signed contribution statement, individual analysis and individual reflection. | Class teacher completes the initial check. The moderation teacher independently marks the moderation sample. | Final marking occurs, but any checkpoint cap remains. |

A submitted draft may receive feedback, but feedback does not remove a cap caused by a missing checkpoint.

## 4. Absence pathway

| Absence circumstance | Required pathway |
|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | The student must not use group data they did not collect. The teacher supplies a named secondary dataset and records authorisation for that student. The individual analysis and reflection use only that dataset. The shared report’s contribution statement records the absence and any later contribution actually completed. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | The student remains on the standard 30-mark portfolio. The teacher provides missed materials and, where necessary, authorised secondary data. Checkpoints remain applicable; a missed checkpoint attracts the stated cap. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | The student remains on the standard portfolio with teacher-provided catch-up materials and authorised secondary data where personal collection was not possible. Individual work cannot use uncollected group data. The student’s report contribution must be recorded accurately. |
| **Absent for more than 2 weeks** | The student must be offered an alternative individual-only task worth **30 marks**, using supplied secondary data. The same eight mark weights are used: 4+4+4+4+4+4+3+3. The first three criteria are interpreted as individual investigation/report evidence rather than group evidence; no group contribution statement is required for the replacement task. Equivalent drafts are required at the two checkpoints where practicable, with the same missing-draft caps. |

## 5. Authenticity protocol

1. Every group report includes a contribution statement signed by all group members. It names the person or people responsible for each section and records absences.
2. The teacher verifies statements against practical attendance, checkpoint drafts, version history, observed work and brief individual confirmations. Conflicting accounts are investigated before the relevant evidence is accepted.
3. Secondary data is supplied or identified by the teacher for a named student. The teacher records the dataset, student and authorisation. The student identifies the dataset in the analysis. A student absent from the practical session is not authorised to use that group’s uncollected data.
4. Plagiarism is checked by comparing drafts, group submissions, wording, data and explanations, followed by a student explanation where needed. Unattributed copied material receives no credit; marks are based only on verified original work. If the individual analysis is found to be plagiarised, the blocking rule applies.

## 6. Moderation procedure

- Two teachers independently mark at least **20% of portfolios: 24 of 120 students**, with six portfolios sampled from each class where possible.
- Both teachers mark the complete portfolio and every criterion independently before discussing results.
- A discrepancy is the absolute difference between teachers’ marks. A difference **greater than 3 marks on any criterion** triggers remoderation.
- For remoderation, both teachers review the evidence and descriptor independently, record revised marks and then confer. The designated moderation lead teacher sets the final moderated mark if disagreement remains, records the reason and applies the agreed interpretation consistently to comparable portfolios.
- The moderation lead teacher makes final moderation, authenticity and blocking decisions.

## 7. Blocking decisions

A portfolio cannot receive **Proficient or Exemplary overall** if:

- **The individual analysis task is missing:** there is no valid individual evidence of analysis.
- **The individual analysis is plagiarised:** the submitted reasoning cannot be treated as the student’s own evidence.
- **The individual analysis is based on data the student neither personally collected nor was authorised to use:** the analysis is not authenticated. This includes an absent practical-session student using group data they did not collect.

An authorised supplied secondary dataset satisfies the evidence requirement. A missed checkpoint is not itself an overall block; it limits the relevant criterion’s maximum mark.
