# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Cohort:** 120 students in 4 classes  
**Portfolio total:** 30 marks

## 3. Checkpoint schedule

| Timing | Submission | Checked by | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1, Day 5** | Group practical plan; individual analysis outline | Class teacher checks group plan and each individual outline. | G1 cannot receive full marks if the group plan is missing. I1 cannot receive full marks if the individual analysis outline is missing. Maximum available band is Proficient. |
| **Week 2, Day 5** | Group practical draft results; individual analysis draft | Class teacher checks draft data/results and each individual draft. | G2 cannot receive full marks if group draft results are missing. I1 and I2 cannot receive full marks if the individual analysis draft is missing. Maximum available band is Proficient. |
| **Week 3, Day 5** | Full portfolio: group practical report, contribution statement, individual analysis task, individual reflection | Class teacher collects and verifies completion. | Work not submitted cannot be assessed beyond the authenticated evidence available. A student who is present but does not submit the required checkpoint material is treated as having missed that checkpoint. Approved absence arrangements must still produce authenticated work by the agreed school deadline; otherwise the relevant full-mark cap applies.

## 4. Absence pathways

| Absence circumstance | Required pathway |
|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | The student must not use group data that they did not collect. Their individual analysis and reflection must use supplied secondary data authorised by the teacher. The group contribution statement must accurately show that the student was absent from data collection. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | Student completes available group and individual work on return. If the missed week included the practical session, individual analysis and reflection use authorised supplied secondary data. Checkpoint evidence must still be submitted through an agreed catch-up arrangement. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | Student completes the remaining accessible work individually where necessary. Any individual analysis or reflection based on missed practical work must use authorised supplied secondary data, not uncollected group data. The teacher records the data source used. |
| **Absent for more than 2 weeks** | Student is offered an **alternative individual-only task worth 30 marks**, using supplied secondary data. It includes an individual evidence investigation report worth 12 marks, individual analysis worth 12 marks, and individual reflection worth 6 marks. The group practical report is replaced; no group contribution statement is required. |

## 5. Authenticity protocol

### Contribution statements
- Each group submits one signed statement naming every group member and identifying who completed planning, equipment setup, data collection, results processing, explanation, report writing, and editing.
- The teacher verifies statements against checkpoint submissions, observed practical participation, group discussion records, and brief individual confirmation where needed.
- Inaccurate statements are corrected before final marking. A student cannot claim practical data collection they did not complete.

### Authorised secondary-data use
- The teacher supplies and records the approved secondary dataset for absent students.
- The individual analysis and reflection must identify the dataset as supplied secondary data.
- Students absent from the practical session may not use group data they did not collect unless the teacher explicitly authorises that use. Authorisation is recorded with the portfolio.

### Plagiarism detection and handling
- Teachers compare final work with checkpoint drafts, contribution statements, student explanations, and other portfolios.
- Where authenticity is uncertain, the student is asked to explain their data, reasoning, wording, and conclusions.
- Material that cannot be authenticated is not credited. Confirmed plagiarised individual analysis is treated as invalid individual evidence and triggers the relevant blocking decision.

---

## 6. Moderation procedure

1. **Sampling rate:** At least **20% of portfolios** are independently marked by two teachers. For 120 students, this is at least **24 portfolios**.
2. **Independent marking:** Both teachers mark the same sampled portfolios independently using the criterion table before comparing marks.
3. **Discrepancy threshold:** A difference of **more than 3 marks on any criterion** triggers remoderation for that portfolio.
4. **Remoderation steps:**  
   - Both teachers review the relevant evidence and descriptor.  
   - They re-mark the disputed criterion independently.  
   - They discuss the evidence and record the agreed mark and reason.  
   - If agreement is not reached, the designated moderation lead reviews the evidence and makes the final criterion decision.  
   - Where a repeated pattern is found, the moderation lead expands the sample for that criterion or class.
5. **Final decisions:** The designated moderation lead makes final decisions on unresolved moderation outcomes, authenticity findings, and blocking decisions.

---

## 7. Blocking decisions for Proficient/Exemplary overall

A portfolio cannot be awarded **Proficient or Exemplary overall** if:

1. **The individual analysis task is missing.**  
   *Rationale:* There is no individual evidence of analysis of energy transfer or use of evidence.

2. **The individual analysis task is plagiarised.**  
   *Rationale:* Proficient or Exemplary achievement requires authenticated individual work.

3. **The individual analysis is based on data the student did not personally collect and was not authorised to use.**  
   *Rationale:* The analysis cannot demonstrate valid individual scientific reasoning from legitimate evidence.

4. **An absent student uses group practical data they did not collect instead of supplied authorised secondary data.**  
   *Rationale:* This breaches the required absence pathway and invalidates the individual evidence.
