# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Cohort:** 120 students, 4 classes  
**Total:** 30 marks  

Students complete:
- one **shared group practical report** in groups of three (12 marks)
- one **individual analysis task** (12 marks)
- one **individual reflection** (6 marks)

## 5. Authenticity Protocol

### Contribution statements
Each group report includes a statement signed by all members identifying who completed each section, such as planning, apparatus setup, data recording, graphing, conclusion drafting and editing.

The teacher verifies statements by:
- comparing them with checkpoint submissions and classroom observation records;
- checking whether named contributions match each student’s individual analysis and reflection;
- asking brief follow-up questions where contributions are unclear or inconsistent;
- recording any disputed contribution before marking is finalised.

### Authorised secondary-data use
Secondary data may be used only when:
- a student was absent from the relevant practical session or week; or
- the teacher has approved its use as part of an adjusted absence pathway.

The teacher provides or explicitly identifies the data set, records the student authorised to use it, and requires the student to label it as “supplied secondary data” in the analysis. Students may not present uncollected group data as their own.

### Plagiarism detection and handling
Teachers compare drafts, final work, group reports, individual analyses and reflections for copied wording, identical reasoning, unexplained shared errors or work inconsistent with classroom evidence. Students may be asked to explain their work orally or complete a short supervised clarification task.

Where plagiarism is suspected:
1. the work is reviewed by the teacher;
2. the student is given an opportunity to respond;
3. unsupported copied material is not credited;
4. a replacement supervised or individual task may be set where appropriate;
5. confirmed plagiarism triggers the relevant blocking decision.

---

## 6. Moderation Procedure

- At least **20% of portfolios** are independently marked by two teachers. For 120 students, this is at least **24 portfolios**.
- The sample includes work from all four classes and a range of achievement levels, including absence-adjusted portfolios where available.
- Each teacher marks sampled portfolios independently using the criterion table.
- If marks differ by **more than 3 marks on any criterion**, that criterion is remoderated.
- Remoderation steps:
  1. teachers compare interpretations of the descriptor and evidence;
  2. teachers agree on an annotated benchmark judgement;
  3. affected portfolios are rechecked against the agreed interpretation;
  4. any required mark changes are recorded.
- The **lead teacher for the depth study**, after consultation with the marking teachers, makes final decisions on disputed marks, authenticity findings and blocking decisions.

---

## 7. Blocking Decisions

A portfolio cannot be awarded an overall **Proficient** or **Exemplary** result if any of the following applies:

1. **Individual analysis task is missing.**  
   *Rationale:* There is no individual evidence of data interpretation and scientific reasoning.

2. **Individual analysis task is plagiarised.**  
   *Rationale:* The result cannot represent the student’s own achievement.

3. **Individual analysis is based on data the student did not personally collect or was not authorised to use.**  
   *Rationale:* The analysis does not meet the required conditions for valid individual evidence.

4. **Required contribution statement is missing, unsigned, or found to be unreliable without resolution.**  
   *Rationale:* The group report cannot be confidently attributed or used as authenticated group evidence.

5. **Required checkpoint evidence is missing and no authorised absence adjustment applies.**  
   *Rationale:* Full achievement cannot be verified because development of the work was not evidenced at the required stages.
