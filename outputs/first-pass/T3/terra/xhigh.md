# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Portfolio total:** 30 marks  

Each group of three submits one shared practical report. Each student also submits an individual analysis task and an individual reflection. The shared practical-report mark is recorded as group evidence; the analysis and reflection are individually marked.

**Band scoring key:**

- For 3-mark criteria: Emerging = 0; Developing = 1; Proficient = 2; Exemplary = 3.

## 2. Mark-allocation check

| Component | Criteria | Total |
|---|---|---:|
| Group practical report | G1 4 + G2 4 + G3 4 | 12 |
| Individual analysis | A1 4 + A2 4 + A3 4 | 12 |
| Individual reflection | R1 3 + R2 3 | 6 |
| **Portfolio total** |  | **30** |

- Total marks = **30**.  
- Highest single criterion = **4 marks**, so no criterion exceeds 8 marks.  
- Required component split = **12 / 12 / 6**.

**Overall portfolio band:** Emerging 0–14; Developing 15–20; Proficient 21–25; Exemplary 26–30, subject to the blocking decisions below.

## 3. Checkpoint schedule

| Date | Submission | Checked by | Consequence if missing at checkpoint |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1, Day 5 | Group practical plan; individual analysis outline | Class teacher checks the group plan and each student’s outline. | Missing group plan: G1 capped at 3/4 for all group members. Missing individual outline: A1 capped at 3/4 for that student. |
| Week 2, Day 5 | Group practical draft results; individual analysis draft, including data processing and a provisional explanation | Class teacher checks group results and each individual draft. | Missing group draft results: G2 capped at 3/4 for all group members. Missing individual analysis draft: A2 and A3 each capped at 3/4 for that student. |
| Week 3, Day 5 | Full portfolio: shared practical report, signed contribution statement, individual analysis and reflection | Class teacher marks; selected portfolios proceed to moderation. | Work is marked only on evidence submitted. Checkpoint caps remain in place. |

A group submission counts as present only when the group submits it by the checkpoint. An individual analysis checkpoint must be submitted by that individual.

## 4. Absence pathway

| Absence circumstance | Required pathway |
|---|---|
| Absent for the practical session | The contribution statement records the absence and identifies work completed by present members. The absent student’s individual analysis and reflection must use supplied secondary data, not group data they did not collect. |
| Absent for 1 week | Student rejoins the normal portfolio pathway. Any data missed are replaced by teacher-authorised supplied secondary data. Checkpoint caps apply if the relevant individual or group draft was not submitted by its checkpoint. |
| Absent for 2 weeks | Student remains on the normal pathway where possible, with teacher-supported catch-up. Individual evidence may use personally collected data and supplied secondary data, but not uncollected group data. Checkpoint rules still apply. |
| Absent for more than 2 weeks | Student must be offered an alternative **individual-only 30-mark task** using supplied secondary data. It replaces the group report with an individual secondary-data practical report worth 12 marks, plus the 12-mark analysis and 6-mark reflection. Two equivalent draft checkpoints are set in writing. |

## 5. Authenticity protocol

- Every group practical report includes a section-by-section contribution statement for planning, practical work/data collection, results, and report completion. All three members sign it, including any member whose recorded contribution is “absent for practical session.”
- The teacher verifies statements against attendance records, checkpoint submissions, observed classroom participation, and brief individual questioning about methods, results and decisions.
- Secondary data are authorised by the teacher through a recorded allocation identifying the student, reason for use, and supplied data set. Students label this source in their analysis and reflection.
- Authorisation to use supplied secondary data does not permit an absent student to use group data they did not collect.
- Teachers compare drafts, final work and individual submissions for copied wording, duplicated reasoning or inconsistent data provenance. Shared wording is expected only in the shared group report.
- Where plagiarism is suspected, the student is asked to explain the work and provide authenticated evidence. Confirmed copied material receives no credit; only verified work is marked.

## 6. Moderation procedure

1. At least **20% of portfolios** are independently marked by two teachers: for 120 students, a minimum of **24 portfolios**, sampled across all four classes.
2. The second teacher marks selected portfolios without seeing the first teacher’s scores.
3. A discrepancy of **more than 3 marks on any criterion** triggers remoderation for that criterion.
4. The two teachers review the evidence and descriptors, then re-mark the disputed criterion. If unresolved, the designated portfolio lead teacher independently adjudicates.
5. Any clarified interpretation is applied by rechecking the affected criterion across relevant portfolios.
6. The portfolio lead teacher confirms final marks, overall bands and blocking decisions.

## 7. Blocking decisions

A portfolio cannot receive an overall **Proficient** or **Exemplary** result if:

| Blocking condition | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Individual analysis task is missing | There is no individual analytical evidence. |
| Individual analysis is confirmed as plagiarised | The work cannot be accepted as the student’s own evidence. |
| Individual analysis uses data the student neither personally collected nor was authorised to use | The evidence cannot be verified as legitimately available to that student. This includes group data used by a student absent from the practical session. |

Where a blocking condition applies, the portfolio may be awarded only Emerging or Developing overall, regardless of raw total.
