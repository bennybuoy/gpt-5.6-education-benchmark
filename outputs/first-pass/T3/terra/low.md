# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Cohort:** 120 students across four classes  
**Portfolio total:** 30 marks  

## 1. Criterion table

| Component and criterion | Marks | Emerging | Developing | Proficient | Exemplary |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| **Group practical report: Investigation plan and method** | 6 | **Limited plan**: States a basic aim or method, with unclear variables, circuit setup or safety considerations. | **Developing plan**: Identifies an aim and workable method with some relevant controls, equipment or safety steps. | **Sound plan**: Clearly designs a fair investigation, identifies relevant variables, gives a replicable circuit method and includes appropriate safety actions. | **Rigorous plan**: Produces a precise, justified and replicable investigation design that anticipates limitations, controls variables effectively and integrates safety throughout. |
| **Group practical report: Results, explanation and conclusion** | 6 | **Limited evidence**: Records incomplete results or gives an explanation/conclusion that is unsupported or inaccurate. | **Developing evidence**: Presents some results and gives a broadly relevant explanation or conclusion with limited use of evidence. | **Sound evidence**: Organises results clearly, identifies patterns and explains energy transfer in the circuit using evidence to support a conclusion. | **Insightful evidence**: Presents accurate, well-organised results; analyses patterns and anomalies; and gives a precise, evidence-based explanation and justified conclusion. |
| **Individual analysis task: Analysis of authorised data and reasoning** | 6 | **Limited analysis**: Makes simple observations from data with little or inaccurate reasoning. | **Developing analysis**: Identifies some patterns and uses partly correct reasoning to interpret authorised data. | **Sound analysis**: Analyses authorised data accurately, identifies relevant relationships and explains reasoning about energy transfer in the circuit. | **Insightful analysis**: Interprets authorised data thoroughly, distinguishes meaningful patterns from anomalies and develops clear, scientifically reasoned explanations. |
| **Individual analysis task: Conclusion, evaluation and communication** | 6 | **Limited response**: Gives a brief conclusion with little evaluation, unclear communication or minimal evidence. | **Developing response**: Gives a relevant conclusion and identifies a simple limitation or improvement, with some evidence. | **Sound response**: Communicates a clear evidence-based conclusion, evaluates data quality or method limitations, and proposes a practical improvement. | **Insightful response**: Delivers a coherent, well-supported conclusion; evaluates the strength and limits of the evidence; and proposes specific, justified improvements. |
| **Individual reflection: Contribution, learning and next steps** | 6 | **Limited reflection**: Gives vague statements about group work or learning, with little personal evidence. | **Developing reflection**: Describes a contribution and learning point, with a general next step. | **Thoughtful reflection**: Explains personal contribution and learning using specific examples, and identifies a realistic next step. | **Critical reflection**: Evaluates personal contribution, collaboration and learning with specific evidence, and sets a precise, actionable improvement goal. |

## 4. Absence pathways

| Absence circumstance | Required pathway |
|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | The student must not use group data they did not collect. Their individual analysis and reflection use teacher-supplied secondary data. The group contribution statement records the absence and identifies work completed before or after the practical session. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | The student completes available group work and submits individual work using personally collected data where possible. For missed practical evidence, the teacher may authorise supplied secondary data. Catch-up checkpoint access is provided where feasible. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | The student completes available components through a combination of authenticated personal work and teacher-authorised supplied secondary data. The teacher records which data sources are authorised for the individual analysis. |
| **Absent for more than 2 weeks** | The student is offered an **alternative individual-only 30-mark task** using supplied secondary data. It includes investigation planning, data interpretation, explanation, evaluation and reflection, with no criterion exceeding 8 marks. Checkpoint requirements are adapted to the student’s attendance timeline. |

## 5. Authenticity protocol

### Contribution statements
Each group practical report must include a statement signed by all members. It must identify who completed each section, such as planning, setup, measurement recording, results presentation, explanation and editing.

The teacher verifies statements through:
- observation notes during practical work;
- comparison with checkpoint drafts;
- checking consistency between named contributions and submitted work; and
- brief individual questioning where contribution is unclear or disputed.

### Authorising secondary data
Secondary data may be used only when supplied or explicitly authorised by the teacher. The teacher records:
- why secondary data were required;
- the source set supplied to the student; and
- whether the student may use it for the individual analysis and reflection.

Students absent from practical data collection must use supplied secondary data rather than group data they did not collect.

### Detecting and handling plagiarism
Teachers compare final work with checkpoints, other student work and expected individual explanations. They may use brief follow-up questions to confirm understanding and authorship.

Where plagiarism is suspected:
1. the teacher gathers relevant evidence and discusses it with the student;
2. unauthenticated material receives no credit until authorship is established;
3. the student may be required to provide an authenticated replacement response where appropriate; and
4. a plagiarised or missing individual analysis task blocks Proficient or Exemplary overall performance.

## 6. Moderation procedure

- At least **20% of portfolios** are moderated: for 120 students, this is at least **24 portfolios**.
- The moderation sample includes work from all four classes and a spread of achievement levels.
- Two teachers independently mark the same sampled portfolios using the criterion table.
- If marks differ by **more than 3 marks on any criterion**, that criterion is remoderated.
- Remoderation steps:
  1. teachers review the relevant evidence and descriptors;
  2. teachers identify the source of the difference;
  3. they agree on an aligned interpretation and recheck comparable portfolios if needed;
  4. unresolved cases are reviewed by the assessment coordinator or nominated moderation lead.
- The assessment coordinator or nominated moderation lead makes final decisions after considering the evidence and moderation record.

## 7. Blocking decisions for Proficient/Exemplary overall

A portfolio cannot be awarded **Proficient** or **Exemplary overall** if:

1. **The individual analysis task is missing.**  
   **Rationale:** There is no individual evidence of data analysis and reasoning.

2. **The individual analysis task is plagiarised or cannot be authenticated as the student’s work.**  
   **Rationale:** Higher overall performance requires verified individual understanding.

3. **The individual analysis uses data the student did not personally collect and was not authorised to use.**  
   **Rationale:** The analysis must be based on legitimate, traceable evidence—either personally collected data or teacher-authorised supplied secondary data.

4. **Required checkpoint evidence is missing for a criterion.**  
   **Rationale:** Missing drafts prevent full marks in the corresponding criterion and limit the evidence available to establish sustained, authenticated achievement.
