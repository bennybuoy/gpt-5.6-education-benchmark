# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture

**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Cohort:** 120 students in four classes  
**Grouping:** Groups of three for the practical report  
**Total:** 30 marks

## 1. Criterion and Performance-Band Table

**Band mark ranges**

- **4-mark criteria:** Emerging 0–1; Developing 2; Proficient 3; Exemplary 4  
- **6-mark criteria:** Emerging 0–1; Developing 2–3; Proficient 4–5; Exemplary 6  
- **3-mark criteria:** Emerging 0; Developing 1; Proficient 2; Exemplary 3

|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|

| **2. **Overall reporting bands:** Emerging 0–11; Developing 12–17; Proficient 18–23; Exemplary 24–30, subject to the blocking decisions below.

## 3. Checkpoint Schedule

| Date | Submission | Checked by | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1, Day 5** | Group practical plan; each student’s individual analysis outline | Class teacher, recorded in checkpoint register | Group plan: Criterion 1 capped at **3/4**. Individual outline: Criterion 5 capped at **5/6**. |
| **Week 2, Day 5** | Group draft results; each student’s individual analysis draft | Class teacher, with brief written or coded feedback | Group results: Criterion 2 capped at **3/4**. Individual draft: Criterion 4 capped at **5/6**. |
| **Week 3, Day 5** | Full portfolio, including signed contribution statement | Class teacher; moderation sample sent to two independent markers | Late or incomplete work is assessed only from evidence submitted. Authenticity blocks are applied before an overall band is awarded. |

A checkpoint counts only when substantive work is submitted. A blank file, title page or undeveloped placeholder is treated as missing. Missing both checkpoints activates both relevant caps.

---

## 4. Absence Pathways

| Absence | Required pathway |
|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | The student may remain named in the group report only for sections they genuinely complete. Their individual analysis and reflection must use a teacher-supplied secondary dataset, not group data collected during their absence. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | Complete the standard portfolio using work personally completed. If the practical was missed, use the practical-absence pathway. Missed checkpoint drafts remain subject to the stated criterion caps. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | Complete the standard portfolio only where contribution and authorship can be authenticated. The teacher supplies secondary data for missed collection. The student may not claim unobserved group work. Missed checkpoint caps still apply. |
| **Absent for more than 2 weeks** | Offer an **individual-only 30-mark alternative** using supplied secondary data: individual investigation report/design and method critique, 12 marks; individual data analysis, 12 marks; individual reflection, 6 marks. Equivalent draft checks and authenticity requirements apply; no group contribution statement is required. |

## 5. Authenticity Protocol

### Contribution statements
- Every group report includes a statement naming each section and the student or students who completed it.
- All three members sign the same statement.
- The teacher checks it against practical observations, checkpoint submissions, draft/version records and brief individual questioning.
- Conflicting claims must be resolved before the group report is accepted as authenticated.

### Secondary-data authorisation
- The teacher issues each approved dataset with a dataset code and records the recipient and reason for use.
- The student identifies the code in the analysis and reflection.
- Data from a practical missed by the student cannot be treated as personally collected. In that situation, group data cannot replace the supplied secondary dataset.

### Plagiarism
- Teachers compare checkpoints with final work, compare unusually similar student responses and use brief oral verification where authorship is uncertain.
- Students are given an opportunity to explain or identify sources of disputed material.
- Confirmed plagiarism receives no credit for the unauthenticated evidence. A plagiarised individual analysis is recorded as invalid and activates the overall-band block.

---

## 6. Moderation Procedure

1. **Sample:** At least **20% of 120 portfolios = 24 portfolios**. The sample includes six from each class and represents different mark ranges and absence/data pathways.
2. **Independent marking:** Two teachers mark the same sample independently, using clean copies and without seeing each other’s scores.
3. **Threshold:** A difference of **more than 3 marks on any criterion** triggers remoderation.
4. **Remoderation:** Both teachers reread the evidence and descriptor, record reasons, and re-mark the disputed criterion. Comparable portfolios in the affected class are checked for consistency.
5. **Unresolved discrepancy:** A designated moderation lead reviews the evidence, both rationales and comparable scripts.
6. **Final decisions:** The moderation lead determines the final criterion score and authorises any necessary adjustment across the affected marking set.

---

## 7. Blocking Decisions

A portfolio cannot receive **Proficient or Exemplary overall** when:

1. **The individual analysis is missing.**  
   *Rationale:* There is no individual evidence of data analysis or scientific reasoning.

2. **The individual analysis is plagiarised.**  
   *Rationale:* The evidence cannot establish the student’s own achievement.

3. **The individual analysis uses data the student neither personally collected nor was authorised to use.**  
   *Rationale:* The analysis lacks valid provenance. Students absent from the practical must use the supplied secondary dataset, not uncollected group data.

Checkpoint omissions create criterion caps rather than an additional automatic overall-band block.
