# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Cohort:** 120 students in 4 classes  
**Total:** 30 marks

## 1. Criterion and Performance-Band Table

| Criterion | Component | Marks | Emerging | Developing | Proficient | Exemplary |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| **Practical planning and method** | Group practical | 6 | **0–1:** Plan is incomplete or unsafe; variables, equipment or method are unclear. | **2–3:** Provides a workable plan with some variables, equipment and safety considerations identified. | **4–5:** Provides a clear, safe and repeatable method with appropriate variables, equipment and controls. | **6:** Provides a precise, logically sequenced and repeatable plan that clearly controls relevant variables and risks. |
| **Practical results and report** | Group practical | 6 | **0–1:** Results are missing, unclear or unsupported; report has little connection to the investigation. | **2–3:** Records some relevant results and gives a basic account of patterns or findings. | **4–5:** Presents accurate, organised results and explains findings using evidence from the practical. | **6:** Presents complete, well-organised results and gives a precise, evidence-based explanation, including limitations or anomalies. |
| **Data processing and pattern identification** | Individual analysis | 6 | **0–1:** Uses little authorised data or processes data inaccurately; patterns are not identified. | **2–3:** Processes some authorised data and identifies an evident pattern with partial accuracy. | **4–5:** Accurately processes authorised data and identifies relevant patterns using appropriate evidence. | **6:** Processes data accurately and thoroughly, identifying and clearly explaining patterns, anomalies and relationships. |
| **Scientific reasoning and conclusions** | Individual analysis | 6 | **0–1:** Conclusion is missing, unsupported or inconsistent with the data. | **2–3:** Gives a basic conclusion with limited reference to data and energy transfer. | **4–5:** Gives a logical conclusion supported by data and clearly explains energy transfer in the circuit. | **6:** Gives a coherent, well-supported conclusion that integrates evidence, energy-transfer reasoning, limitations and possible improvements. |
| **Individual reflection on learning and contribution** | Individual reflection | 6 | **0–1:** Gives minimal or unsupported comments about learning or contribution. | **2–3:** Describes own contribution and some learning, with limited supporting examples. | **4–5:** Explains own contribution, learning and challenges using specific examples and identifies a realistic improvement. | **6:** Critically evaluates own contribution, learning, decisions and challenges, using specific evidence and proposing well-justified improvements. |

### Overall reporting bands

- **Emerging:** 0–11  
- **Developing:** 12–17  
- **Proficient:** 18–23  
- **Exemplary:** 24–30  

Overall bands remain subject to the blocking decisions in Section 7.

- Highest value of any criterion: **6 marks**
- No criterion exceeds the maximum of **8 marks**.
- The shared practical report receives one group mark out of 12, subject to verified contribution and authenticity evidence.

| Time | Submission | Who checks it | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1, Day 5** | Group practical plan, including proposed method, variables, equipment and safety; each student’s individual analysis outline | Class teacher; group plan is checked with the group and analysis outline with each student | The group cannot receive **6/6** for Practical planning and method; maximum **5/6**. The student cannot receive **6/6** for Scientific reasoning and conclusions; maximum **5/6**. Teacher records the missing draft and requires submission before further feedback. |
| **Week 2, Day 5** | Group draft results, including tables/observations; each student’s individual analysis draft | Class teacher; individual drafts are checked separately | The group cannot receive **6/6** for Practical results and report; maximum **5/6**. The student cannot receive **6/6** for Data processing and pattern identification; maximum **5/6**. Teacher records the missing draft and may provide only general, not draft-specific, feedback. |
| **Week 3, Day 5** | Full portfolio: signed group practical report, individual analysis and individual reflection | Class teacher, followed by moderation sample | Work is marked only from evidence submitted. Missing components receive no marks for their criteria. A missing individual analysis also activates the overall band block. |

An absence recorded by the teacher does not remove a checkpoint requirement. The teacher sets an adjusted submission opportunity where needed, using the relevant absence pathway.

## 4. Absence Pathways

| Absence circumstance | Evidence pathway | Submission and marking arrangements |
|---|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | Student must not claim or analyse group data they did not personally collect. Teacher supplies an identified secondary-data set. | Student may contribute to authorised non-data sections of the group report. Their individual analysis and reflection must use and acknowledge the supplied secondary data. Reflection focuses on analysis, learning and authorised contribution rather than claiming practical participation. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | Student resumes the standard portfolio where they have personally participated. For any missed data collection, the teacher authorises supplied secondary data. | Teacher provides a reasonable adjusted checkpoint opportunity. Group contribution statement identifies completed and missed sections. Standard 30-mark structure remains. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | Teacher identifies which evidence can validly be completed. Supplied secondary data is used for missed practical evidence; group data may be used only if the student personally helped collect it. | Student completes individual analysis and reflection plus only genuinely completed group-report sections. The teacher may assign an individual version of missed practical-report work using supplied data while retaining the **12/12/6** allocation. Adjusted checkpoints are documented. |
| **Absent for more than 2 of the 3 weeks** | Student is offered an **individual-only alternative task worth 30 marks**, entirely based on teacher-supplied secondary data. | The alternative assesses equivalent areas: practical planning/reporting for 12 marks, analysis for 12 marks and reflection for 6 marks. It uses the same criterion values and performance bands, adapted from “group contribution” to individual decision-making. |

## 5. Authenticity Protocol

### Contribution statements
Each group report includes a table naming every section and identifying who planned, drafted, checked and revised it. All three members sign the statement. The teacher verifies it through:

- checkpoint submissions and dated draft records;
- observation notes from the practical;
- brief individual questions about methods, results and assigned sections;
- comparison of the statement with available editing or version records.

A signature alone is not sufficient if other evidence contradicts the claimed contribution. Disputed claims are checked through separate student conferences.

### Authorised secondary data
Secondary data must be:

1. supplied or explicitly approved by the teacher;
2. labelled with a data-set identifier;
3. acknowledged in the analysis and reflection;
4. recorded by the teacher on an authorisation log, including the reason for use.

Data from another group is not automatically authorised. Students may not present data as personally collected when they were absent.

### Plagiarism
Teachers compare final work with checkpoint drafts, contribution records, other submissions and students’ ability to explain their reasoning. Suspected copying triggers an individual conference. The student is shown the concern and given an opportunity to identify sources and explain authorship.

If plagiarism is substantiated, only authenticated evidence is marked. A plagiarised individual analysis activates the overall Proficient/Exemplary block. Any permitted replacement submission must be clearly recorded and independently completed.

## 6. Moderation Procedure

1. **Sampling:** Two teachers independently mark at least **20% of portfolios**—at least **24 of 120**. The sample includes all four classes and a spread of preliminary achievement levels and absence pathways.
2. **Independent marking:** Each teacher applies the criterion descriptors without seeing the other teacher’s marks.
3. **Threshold:** A difference of **more than 3 marks on any one criterion** triggers remoderation.
4. **Remoderation:**  
   - Both markers re-read the criterion and evidence independently.  
   - They record the evidence supporting their mark.  
   - A third teacher reviews that criterion without seeing the preferred outcome.  
   - Comparable portfolios from the affected class or performance range are checked to determine whether the discrepancy is isolated or systematic.  
   - If systematic, the relevant criterion is remoderated across all affected portfolios.
5. **Final decisions:** The class teacher and second marker seek an evidence-based agreement. Where agreement is not reached, the third teacher makes the final criterion decision. Blocking decisions are confirmed jointly by the class teacher and moderation lead.

---

## 7. Blocking Decisions

A portfolio cannot receive an overall **Proficient or Exemplary** band if:

1. **The individual analysis task is missing.**  
   *Rationale:* There is no individual evidence of data processing or scientific reasoning.

2. **The individual analysis is plagiarised.**  
   *Rationale:* The work does not provide authenticated evidence of the student’s achievement.

3. **The individual analysis uses data the student did not personally collect and was not authorised to use.**  
   *Rationale:* Conclusions are based on invalid or unauthorised evidence.

4. **A student absent from the practical presents group data as personally collected.**  
   *Rationale:* This is an authenticity breach and makes the individual analysis unauthorised.

These blocks limit the overall result to **Developing**, regardless of the numerical total, until any formally permitted and authenticated replacement evidence is assessed.
