# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks | **Cohort:** 120 students, 4 classes  
**Total:** 30 marks

One group report is submitted per group of three. Its criterion marks are shared by the group and entered in each member’s portfolio. Analysis and reflection marks are individual.

## 1. Criterion and Performance-Band Table

For each 4-mark criterion: Emerging = 1, Developing = 2, Proficient = 3, Exemplary = 4. No assessable evidence receives 0. For the 6-mark reflection, the available ranges are shown.

Investigation plan and circuit method** | Group practical | 4 | Names an aim or components, but the method is fragmentary or unclear. | Gives a workable sequence and circuit plan; some variables or measurements are unclear. | Provides a clear, repeatable method identifying what is changed, measured and controlled. | Provides a precise, justified method with suitable controls, repeated measurements and clear links to energy transfer. |

## 3. Checkpoint Schedule

| Date | Submission | Checked by | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1, Day 5** | One group practical plan; one individual analysis outline per student | Class teacher, recorded in checkpoint log | Missing group plan: G1 capped at 3/4. Missing individual outline: A1 capped at 3/4. |
| **Week 2, Day 5** | Group draft results; individual analysis draft including processing and tentative interpretation | Class teacher, recorded in checkpoint log | Missing group results draft: G2 capped at 3/4. Missing individual draft: A2 and A3 each capped at 3/4. |
| **Week 3, Day 5** | Full report, signed contribution statement, individual analysis and individual reflection | Class teacher; sampled portfolios enter moderation | Missing evidence receives 0 for affected criteria. A missing individual analysis also activates an overall-band block. |

Checkpoint caps apply when the required draft is not submitted by the checkpoint, including where absence causes the checkpoint to be missed.

## 4. Absence Pathways

| Absence | Required pathway |
|---|---|
| **Absent for practical session** | The contribution statement records the absence and any later non-collection contribution. The student must use teacher-authorised supplied secondary data for their analysis and reflection, not the group data collected while they were absent. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | Completes the standard portfolio. If the practical was missed, the practical-session pathway applies. Any checkpoint actually missed attracts the stated cap. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | Completes the standard portfolio using authorised secondary data for sessions in which data were not personally collected. Actual group contribution is recorded accurately. Missed-checkpoint caps still apply. |
| **Absent for more than 2 weeks** | Is offered an alternative individual-only task worth 30 marks using supplied secondary data. It retains 12 marks for an individual secondary-data investigation, 12 for analysis and 6 for reflection, using the same quality standards. Two equivalent draft checks are scheduled and recorded when the task is issued. |

## 5. Authenticity Protocol

- **Contribution statements:** Each report lists every section and its contributor and is signed by all group members. The teacher checks it against checkpoint drafts, observed participation and brief individual confirmation. Discrepancies are resolved with all members before marks are finalised.
- **Secondary data:** The teacher records the student, dataset, reason and date in an authorisation log. Students label the dataset as supplied secondary data. A student absent from collection cannot claim or use the group dataset as personally collected data.
- **Plagiarism:** Teachers compare individual tasks with checkpoint drafts, other student responses and the shared report, then ask the student to explain their reasoning where authorship is uncertain. Confirmed copied material is not accepted as authentic evidence. Confirmed plagiarism in the individual analysis activates the overall-band block.

## 6. Moderation Procedure

- At least **24 portfolios (20% of 120)** are independently marked by two teachers, with at least six from each class and, where possible, different practical groups and performance levels.
- Marks are compared criterion by criterion.
- Any discrepancy **greater than 3 marks on a criterion** triggers remoderation of that criterion across the sample.
- Both teachers re-read the evidence independently, apply the descriptors again, compare decisions and establish an agreed interpretation. All cohort marks for the affected criterion are then reviewed for consistency.
- The two teachers seek consensus. If unresolved, the designated moderation lead makes and records the final decision. The moderation lead also confirms final marks and blocking decisions.

## 7. Blocking Decisions

A portfolio cannot receive **Proficient or Exemplary overall**, regardless of its numerical total, when:

1. **The individual analysis is missing** — there is no required individual evidence of analytical understanding.
2. **The individual analysis is plagiarised** — authorship and individual achievement cannot be authenticated.
3. **The analysis uses data the student neither personally helped collect nor was authorised to use** — the evidence has invalid provenance. This includes group data used by a student who was absent during its collection.

Checkpoint caps and a missing contribution statement affect marks or trigger authenticity checking, but are not additional automatic overall-band blocks.
