# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks | **Cohort:** 120 students | **Total:** 30 marks

Students form 40 groups of three. Each standard portfolio contains the group’s shared practical report and the student’s individual analysis and reflection. G1–G3 receive a shared group mark; A1–A2 and R1 are marked individually.

## 1. Criterion table

For 4-mark criteria: Emerging = 1, Developing = 2, Proficient = 3, Exemplary = 4.  
For 6-mark criteria: Emerging = 1, Developing = 2–3, Proficient = 4–5, Exemplary = 6. The upper point is used when performance is consistently complete within the descriptor. **Zero indicates no assessable evidence.**

| Criterion | Marks | Component | Emerging | Developing | Proficient | Exemplary |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| **G1. Investigation planning and method** | 4 | Group practical | **Fragmentary design:** Purpose or method is unclear; variables or data plan largely absent. | **Workable design:** Purpose and basic circuit method are present; controls or data plan are partial. | **Controlled design:** Clear variables and a repeatable method gather relevant energy-transfer evidence. | **Justified design:** Coherent, controlled method with justified choices and anticipated data-quality issues. |
| **G2. Results recording and presentation** | 4 | Group practical | **Sparse record:** Results are incomplete or difficult to interpret. | **Basic record:** Main results are shown, with some suitable organisation, labels or units. | **Clear record:** Relevant results are complete, logically organised and accurately labelled for comparison. | **Rigorous record:** Precise, comprehensive presentation makes patterns, repeats and anomalies readily visible. |
| **G3. Practical conclusion and evaluation** | 4 | Group practical | **Unsupported claim:** Conclusion has little connection to results or energy transfer. | **Descriptive conclusion:** Identifies a general pattern with limited evidence or evaluation. | **Evidence-linked conclusion:** Uses results to explain energy transfer and identifies a relevant limitation or improvement. | **Critical conclusion:** Integrates evidence, limitations and a well-justified improvement into a convincing conclusion. |

Independent data processing and pattern analysis** | 6 | Individual analysis | **Limited analysis:** Minimal processing; patterns are absent, inaccurate or unsupported. | **Partial analysis:** Some valid processing and pattern identification, but with gaps or errors. | **Systematic analysis:** Independently processes permitted data and identifies supported relationships using suitable representations. | **Incisive analysis:** Accurate, efficient processing reveals and substantiates significant patterns, anomalies and relationships. |

**Overall bands:** Emerging 0–11; Developing 12–20; Proficient 21–25; Exemplary 26–30, subject to the blocking decisions below.

## 2. Mark allocation check

| Component | Calculation | Total |
|---|---:|---:|
| Group practical report | G1 4 + G2 4 + G3 4 | **12** |
| Individual analysis | A1 6 + A2 6 | **12** |
| Individual reflection | R1 6 | **6** |
| **Portfolio total** | 12 + 12 + 6 | **30** |

The largest criterion is 6 marks, which does not exceed the 8-mark limit.

## 3. Checkpoint schedule

| Date | Submission | Checked by | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1/Day 5** | One group practical plan; one individual analysis outline per student | Class teacher records receipt and gives brief feedback | Missing group plan: **G1 capped at 3/4** for the group. Missing individual outline: **A1 capped at 5/6** for that student. |
| **Week 2/Day 5** | Group draft results; individual analysis draft | Class teacher | Missing group results draft: **G2 capped at 3/4**. Missing individual draft: **A2 capped at 5/6**. |
| **Week 3/Day 5** | Shared final report with signed contribution statement; individual analysis and reflection | Class teacher; moderation teachers for sampled portfolios | A missing component receives zero for its criteria. A missing analysis also activates an overall block. Group marks are not released until the contribution statement is verified. |

A submission not logged by the checkpoint, including a late submission, counts as missing for the full-mark cap. Checkpoints carry no separate marks.

## 4. Absence pathways

| Absence | Pathway |
|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | Remains on the standard group-report pathway. The contribution statement records the absence and only actual report contributions. The student’s analysis and reflection must use authorised supplied secondary data, not group data collected during the absence. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | Completes the standard portfolio. Personally collected data may be used only from attended sessions. If the practical was missed, the practical-session rule applies. Any missed checkpoint cap remains in force. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | Still completes the standard portfolio because the alternative threshold is more than two weeks. Uncollected data are replaced with authorised supplied secondary data. Contribution records must show only work actually completed; checkpoint caps apply. |
| **Absent for more than 2 weeks** | Must be offered an **individual-only alternative worth 30 marks**, using supplied secondary data. G1–G3 become an individually authored 12-mark practical-style report; A1–A2 and R1 remain 12 and 6 marks. No group contribution statement is required. |

The alternative uses its own three-week sequence: **Alternative Week 1/Day 5** plan and outline, **Alternative Week 2/Day 5** draft results and analysis, and **Alternative Week 3/Day 5** final submission. The same criterion caps apply to missing drafts.

## 5. Authenticity protocol

- **Contribution statements:** Each shared report includes a section-by-section table naming who completed each section, including planning, data collection, presentation and conclusion. All members sign it. The teacher cross-checks it against checkpoint drafts, practical observations, reflections and brief individual confirmation. Conflicting accounts are checked separately and resolved before group marks are released.
- **Secondary-data authorisation:** The teacher issues an identified data packet and records the student, permitted component, reason and date. Students label data as either personally collected or teacher-supplied and cite the packet identifier. An absent student cannot retrospectively claim group data collected without them.
- **Plagiarism:** Teachers compare individual finals with checkpoint drafts, other individual responses, the shared report, calculation trails and the student’s explanation of their work. Identical supplied raw data are not plagiarism; copied wording, processing or interpretation may be. Suspected copying is documented and the student may explain it. Confirmed copied material receives no credit; confirmed plagiarism in the individual analysis activates the overall block.

## 6. Moderation procedure

1. Two teachers independently mark the same sample of **at least 24 complete portfolios**, representing at least 20% of 120 students and all four classes.
2. They score every criterion independently before discussing results.
3. An absolute difference **greater than 3 marks on any criterion** triggers remoderation of that criterion.
4. Both teachers recheck the evidence, descriptor, checkpoint cap and authenticity record, then independently re-mark. For a group criterion, all linked copies of the shared report are checked.
5. They compare the affected criterion with other sampled portfolios. If the discrepancy reflects a broader interpretation issue, all potentially affected scores for that criterion are reviewed.
6. The two moderation teachers jointly make and record final scoring and blocking decisions. Class teachers apply the agreed interpretation to unsampled portfolios.

## 7. Blocking decisions

Regardless of the numerical total, the overall result cannot exceed **Developing** when:

1. **The individual analysis is missing:** there is no mandatory individual evidence of analytical understanding.
2. **The individual analysis is confirmed as plagiarised:** the evidence cannot be attributed to the student.
3. **The analysis uses unauthorised data:** data were neither personally collected nor expressly authorised, or were used outside the recorded authorisation. This includes group data collected while the student was absent.

The numerical mark remains reported, but it cannot generate a Proficient or Exemplary overall band while a block applies.
