## DELIVERABLE 2 — Corrected Lesson Decisions

### Starter
- Retain the candle only as a teacher-controlled observation if permitted by the school risk assessment.
- Ask students to distinguish **wax melting** from **wax combustion** and to separate observations from inferences.
- Remove the rule that a flame alone proves chemical change; require evidence that new substances form (**F6, F12, F17**).

### Demonstration
- Use the vinegar–bicarbonate reaction to compare **open-system apparent mass change** with conservation of total mass in a suitably closed, school-approved system.
- Explain that carbon dioxide leaving an open container causes the measured mass to fall; mass is not destroyed.
- Define the system boundary and take stable balance readings (**F4, F13**).

### Stations
- Reduce the number of stations or lengthen the rotation.
- Correct the science:
  - Melting ice is a physical change (**F11**).
  - Burning steel wool consumes oxygen and forms iron oxide (**F7**).
  - A red universal-indicator result shows acidity but does not identify hydrochloric acid (**F8**).
  - Rusting still involves activation energy (**F9**).
- Remove the unsafe student magnesium procedure. Any combustion demonstration must be teacher-controlled and follow approved flame, eye, heat, and ventilation precautions (**F1, F6**).
- Use only labelled, risk-assessed, low-hazard solutions—not unrestricted “unknowns” (**F5**).
- Replace answer-giving worksheet statements with observation, evidence, system-boundary, and explanation prompts (**F16**).
- Give a pre-lab safety briefing and state PPE, spill, hot-material, conduct, and handwashing requirements (**F17**).

### Plenary
- Use the exothermic profile to show both:
  - a positive activation-energy barrier, and
  - a lower product energy than reactant energy.
- Emphasise that an exothermic reaction can still need ignition or another initial energy input (**F9, F10**).
- Revisit the candle, mass demonstration, and one station to connect all three objectives.

### Assessment
- Replace the single all-or-nothing true/false question with several brief items covering:
  1. physical versus chemical change,
  2. conservation of mass and open/closed systems,
  3. activation energy on a reaction profile.
- Require at least one short justification or annotated diagram.
- Use the exit ticket diagnostically and award credit for reasoning, not merely selecting “True” (**F14, F15**).

### Timing
- Do not schedule five five-minute stations inside a 20-minute block.
- Either use fewer stations or provide at least 25 minutes plus transition time.
- Include explicit time for instructions, the safety briefing, movement, assessment, and cleanup (**F18, F19**).

### Cleanup
- Never permit tasting of any laboratory material (**F2**).
- Do not combine waste or pour solids and unapproved solutions into the sink.
- Keep waste separated and dispose of it according to school guidance and the relevant risk assessments (**F3**).
- Allow hot materials to cool, account for equipment, clean benches, and require handwashing; allocate more than a nominal final five minutes where necessary (**F6, F17, F20**).
