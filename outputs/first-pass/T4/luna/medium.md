## Deliverable 2 — Corrected Lesson Decisions

- **Starter:** Replace the flame claim with an evidence-sorting question. Use the candle to distinguish wax melting, a physical change, from combustion of wax vapour, a chemical change. Students should justify conclusions using more than the presence of a flame. **(F5, F14)**

- **Demonstration:** Do not claim that mass is destroyed. Use a teacher-controlled, safely closed system or provide suitable data showing that mass is conserved when matter cannot escape. Explain the apparent mass decrease in an open vinegar–baking soda system as escaping carbon dioxide. Protect balances from spills. **(F4, F13)**

- **Stations:** Reduce the number of stations or extend the practical period. Correct the content:
  - melting ice = physical change;
  - burning steel wool consumes oxygen from the air;
  - universal indicator shows approximate pH, not chemical identity;
  - rusting still requires activation energy;
  - magnesium, if retained, is teacher-controlled and never cut over a flame.  
  **(F1, F6, F7, F8, F11, F16, F17)**

- **Safety arrangements:** Begin practical work with a safety briefing covering eye protection, flame safety, supervision, spill response, equipment handling, and stop instructions. Students must not taste any substance. **(F1, F2, F12)**

- **Plenary:** Use the reaction profile to identify activation energy for both exothermic and endothermic reactions. Explain that exothermic means net energy is released, not that activation energy is unnecessary. **(F9)**

- **Assessment:** Replace the single true/false item with a short set of questions assessing classification of changes, conservation of mass in open and closed systems, interpretation of a reaction profile, and explanation of evidence for chemical change. Require brief written reasoning. **(F10)**

- **Timing:** Allocate time for instructions, transitions, recording observations, and cleanup. Use fewer stations or run selected demonstrations while groups complete analysis tasks. **(F11, F15)**

- **Cleanup:** Keep waste streams separate and follow school chemical-disposal procedures. Do not pour mixed station residues into the sink unless specifically authorised by the school’s procedures, and never allow tasting of laboratory materials. **(F2, F3)**
