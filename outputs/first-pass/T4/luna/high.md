## DELIVERABLE 2 — Corrected Lesson Decisions

- **Starter — change the claim** *(F10, F11)*  
  Use the candle only as a teacher-controlled observation. Have students distinguish melting wax, which is physical, from combustion, which forms new substances. Treat the flame as evidence to discuss, not as proof by itself.

- **Demonstration — correct conservation of mass** *(F4, F7, F11)*  
  Do not state that mass is destroyed. Use a safe teacher-approved whole-system demonstration, or use supplied data/model evidence, to show that gas leaving an open system accounts for an apparent mass loss. Explain that steel wool gains mass because oxygen from the air is incorporated into oxide products.

- **Stations — remove unsafe and incorrect tasks** *(F1, F6–F9, F11, F13)*  
  - Classify melting ice as a physical change.  
  - Explain steel wool oxidation as oxygen consumption, not oxygen production.  
  - Use known, teacher-approved samples; interpret red universal indicator as evidence of acidity, not proof of hydrochloric acid.  
  - Remove the instruction to cut or manipulate magnesium over a flame; replace it with a safer teacher demonstration, data set or non-flame activity.  
  - Explain that rusting has an activation-energy requirement and proceeds slowly at room temperature.  
  - Replace worksheet conclusions with observation–evidence–explanation prompts.

- **Plenary — teach activation energy accurately** *(F5, F6)*  
  Use the reaction profile to identify the activation-energy barrier separately from the overall energy change. Emphasise that exothermic reactions can still require an initial energy input.

- **Assessment — align it with the objectives** *(F12, F13)*  
  Replace the single True/False item with short, varied questions: classify a change, explain an open-versus-closed mass result, and interpret activation energy on a reaction profile. Require brief reasoning rather than awarding all credit for one unsubstantiated answer.

- **Timing — make the rotation realistic** *(F14)*  
  Either reduce the number of stations or provide at least the actual station time plus movement, instructions and recording time. Include explicit setup and transition time, and do not use time pressure to justify unsafe handling.

- **Cleanup — no tasting and controlled disposal** *(F2, F3, F15)*  
  Ban tasting. Keep different wastes separate, use the school’s approved disposal route, and have the teacher supervise disposal and equipment checks. Reserve enough time to extinguish or secure demonstrations, collect solids, clean suitable equipment and inspect the room before dismissal.
