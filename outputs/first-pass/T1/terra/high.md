## Unit plan: Energy, circuits and models (10 lessons)

| Lesson | Scheduled day/period | Content statement addressed | Lesson type | Brief activity description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Week 1 Monday P2 | C1 | Theory | Students distinguish a model from the system it represents and identify how a model can simplify explanation or prediction. |
| 2 | Week 1 Tuesday P4 | C2 | Theory | Students trace examples of energy transfer as heat, light, motion and electric current using a teacher-led sorting activity. |
| 3 | Week 1 Wednesday P1 | C3 | Practical | In bench groups, students construct and test simple circuits to determine that a continuous path, source and load are all required. |
| 4 | Week 1 Thursday P3 | C4; formative check of C1–C3 | Formative | Students learn that current is charge flow measured in amperes, then complete a 15-minute circuit-conditions checkpoint. |
| 5 | Week 2 Monday P2 | C5 | Theory | Students interpret voltage as energy transferred per unit charge and use volts as the appropriate measurement unit. |
| 6 | Week 2 Tuesday P4 | C6 | Theory | Students investigate, through diagrams and examples, how material, length, thickness and temperature affect resistance. |
| 7 | Week 2 Wednesday P1 | C7; formative check of C6 | Formative | Students introduce series and parallel pathways, then complete a 15-minute resistance-factor checkpoint. |
| 8 | Week 3 Monday P2 | C7 | Practical | Students build and compare one-path and multiple-path circuits to identify series and parallel circuit behaviour. |
| 9 | Week 3 Tuesday P4 | C8, then C9 | Theory | Students use \(V = IR\) with simple resistive-circuit values and identify when a circuit model’s assumptions can limit its predictions. |
| 10 | Week 3 Wednesday P1 | C7, C8, C9 | Summative | Students complete a 30-minute printed task comparing circuit types, applying \(V = IR\), and evaluating a model limitation. |

## Practical placement justification

### Lesson 3 practical: Continuous circuits
- **Placement:** This practical occurs immediately after C1 and C2 so students can use the idea of energy transfer while learning the C3 requirements of a circuit before C4 introduces current.
- **Equipment and grouping:** The class is arranged into six groups of 5, 5, 5, 5, 4 and 4 students at the six fixed bench stations. Each station uses one power supply, for a total of six, and no multimeters, glassware or hand lenses are required. All 28 students can use the class set of 30 safety goggles.
- **Usable bench time:** The lesson is structured as 5 minutes for safety/setup, 40 minutes for constructing and testing complete and incomplete circuit arrangements, and 5 minutes for pack-up. Materials are teacher-checked before the lesson, so the activity does not rely on technician support.

### Lesson 8 practical: Series and parallel circuits
- **Placement:** This practical follows the Lesson 7 introduction to C7 and occurs before C8, allowing students to observe one-path and multiple-path arrangements before using the simple resistive model quantitatively.
- **Equipment and grouping:** The same six groups use the six fixed stations and one power supply per station, staying within the six-power-supply limit. Students compare circuit arrangements by observation; the four digital multimeters are not required, so no group is disadvantaged by meter availability. All students use the available safety goggles.
- **Usable bench time:** The lesson allows 5 minutes for setup and safety directions, 40 minutes for constructing and comparing series and parallel arrangements, and 5 minutes for pack-up. It is the only practical in Week 3; Lesson 3 is the only practical in Week 1, meeting the maximum of two practical lessons per week.

## Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals if a practical is unavailable
- If the Lesson 3 practical cannot run, **C4 through C9** are deferred until C3 has been taught through the rescheduled practical; the Lesson 4 formative checkpoint also moves.
- If the Lesson 8 practical cannot run, **C8, C9 and the final summative task** are deferred until students have completed the planned C7 comparison practical.
- If the Monday P2 practical slot is lost, the practical is moved to the next available non-Friday lesson before continuing the prerequisite chain.

### Timetable conflicts
- No practical is scheduled on Friday P5.
- The sequence uses one practical in Week 1 and one in Week 3, so it remains below the two-practical-per-week limit.
- The plan does not depend on after-1:00pm technician assistance; practical materials are teacher-checked before class.
- Friday P5 is not used, avoiding early-dismissal risk.

### Risks and mitigation
1. **Equipment or power-supply failure:** Teacher checks the six station setups before class and keeps fault-checking steps on the printed instruction sheet so loose connections can be corrected quickly.
2. **Loss of usable bench time during setup or pack-up:** Equipment is pre-sorted by station, student roles are allocated on entry, and a visible timer protects the 40-minute investigation period.
3. **Unsafe or incorrect circuit configuration:** Students keep power supplies off while constructing circuits, wear goggles, and obtain a teacher check before switching on.
4. **Shared-lab access disruption:** Printed theory and formative materials are retained as a non-practical contingency while the practical is rescheduled before dependent content proceeds.
5. **Student absence during a practical:** Absent students complete the printed circuit-record sheet and join a group during the next available practical catch-up opportunity before the summative task.

## Adjustments

### Lesson 3 practical
- Both students receive printed, numbered circuit-building instructions and a clear circuit diagram at the end of Lesson 2 for advance processing.
- At the bench, the teacher checks each step with them before they connect the source, and they are not required to rotate roles on a fixed timetable.
- Their group records shared observations, allowing the students additional processing time to explain why an arrangement is complete or incomplete.

### Lesson 8 practical
- Both students receive the printed series/parallel procedure and comparison table in Lesson 7 before the practical.
- They receive a teacher check-in before testing each circuit and may use the full bench period to complete the comparison table without a speed requirement.
- A group peer reads aloud the station instruction if requested while each student retains their own printed copy.

### Lesson 4 formative checkpoint
- Both students receive the printed checkpoint format and key vocabulary at the end of Lesson 3 for advance processing.
- During the checkpoint they use their printed instructions, and the teacher provides a brief individual clarification check before marking feedback is returned in the same lesson.

### Lesson 7 formative checkpoint
- Both students receive the printed resistance-factor checklist in Lesson 6 before the checkpoint.
- They use the printed task with extra reading and processing time built through advance access, then receive the same immediate answer-strip and teacher feedback as the class.
