## Unit sequence

| Lesson | Scheduled day/period | Content statement addressed | Lesson type | Brief activity description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Week 1 Monday P2 | C1 | Theory | Students identify a model as a simplifying representation and state how it can explain or predict system behaviour. |
| 2 | Week 1 Tuesday P4 | C2 | Theory | Students sort supplied system representations by energy transfer between systems as heat, light, motion, or electric current. |
| 3 | Week 1 Wednesday P1 | C3 | Practical | Following a concise C3 launch, groups construct continuous and deliberately broken source–load paths and label each required circuit element. |
| 4 | Week 1 Thursday P3 | C3 checkpoint → C4 | Formative/theory | Students complete a 15-minute C3 checkpoint with immediate feedback, then introduce current as charge flow measured in amperes. |
| 5 | Week 1 Friday P5 | C5 | Theory | Students describe voltage as energy transferred per unit charge and use volts on simple circuit representations. |
| 6 | Week 2 Monday P2 | C6 | Theory | Students relate resistance opposing current to material, length, thickness, and temperature. |
| 7 | Week 2 Tuesday P4 | C6 checkpoint → C7 | Formative/theory | Students complete the C6 checkpoint with immediate feedback and then compare one-path and multiple-path circuit models. |
| 8 | Week 2 Wednesday P1 | C7 | Practical | Groups construct, trace, and record series and parallel path models to investigate the one-path/multiple-path distinction. |
| 9 | Week 2 Thursday P3 | C8 → C9 | Theory | Students first model simple resistive circuits quantitatively using \(V = IR\), then identify limits when model assumptions break down. |
| 10 | Week 3 Monday P2 | C7, C8, C9 | Summative | Students complete a 30-minute integrated paper on a supplied simple circuit model by identifying paths, using \(V = IR\), and explaining one model limitation. |

## Practical placement justification

Both practicals use six bench groups of 5, 5, 5, 5, 4 and 4 students. Each group uses one fixed bench station and one power supply, using all 6 available power supplies but not exceeding the limit. All 28 students can use goggles from the class set of 30. Neither practical requires glassware, digital multimeters, hand lenses, the fume cupboard, or mobile devices; observations are recorded on paper.

- **Lesson 3, Week 1 Wednesday P1: C3 practical.** This occurs after C1 and C2 and before the C3 formative checkpoint in Lesson 4, so students establish the required continuous path, source, and load before being checked. Wednesday P1 avoids the after-1:00 pm technician limitation. The timing is 0–5 minutes for pre-positioned goggles, instructions, and setup; 5–45 minutes for bench work; and 45–50 minutes for pack-up, giving 40 usable bench minutes.

- **Lesson 8, Week 2 Wednesday P1: C7 practical.** This follows the C7 introduction in Lesson 7 and directly supports the distinction between series and parallel paths before C8 and C9 are taught. It uses the same six-group, six-power-supply, 28-goggle allocation and again does not use the limited multimeters. The timing is again 0–5 minutes setup, 5–45 minutes bench work, and 45–50 minutes pack-up, providing 40 usable bench minutes. There is one practical in Week 1 and one in Week 2.

## Formative cadence

- **Lesson 4: C3 checkpoint.** This low-stakes, 15-minute paper task asks students to identify a source, load, continuous path, and a break in a supplied circuit representation. Students compare their retained response with a projected answer model, make corrections, and receive a teacher next-step comment before C4 teaching begins in the same lesson.

- **Lesson 7: C6 checkpoint.** This low-stakes, 15-minute paper task checks that students can identify resistance as opposing current and recognise material, length, thickness, and temperature as relevant factors. Students self-correct against a projected answer model while the teacher checks responses and gives a next-step comment before C7 teaching begins in the same lesson.

## Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals
- If **Lesson 3** cannot run, defer the Lesson 4 C3 checkpoint and all subsequent content, C4–C9, until the C3 practical is rescheduled in a suitable non-Friday practical slot.
- If **Lesson 8** cannot run, retain the C7 theory from Lesson 7 but defer C8, C9, and the summative task until a replacement C7/C8 practical is completed.
- If the Friday P5 C5 lesson is shortened, complete C5 first in Lesson 6 and delay C6, its formative checkpoint, and subsequent lessons as needed.

### Timetable conflicts
- Friday P5 is used only for theory; no practical is scheduled there.
- Both practicals are scheduled in Wednesday P1 to avoid the stated after-1:00 pm technician limitation.
- The plan schedules only one practical in each of Weeks 1 and 2, remaining below the maximum of two practicals per week.

### Risks and mitigation
- **Early dismissal risk:** Friday P5 hosts only C5 theory, with a printed task that can be continued at the next lesson.
- **Shared-lab or technician access risk:** Confirm L4 booking, six available benches, and practical setup before each Wednesday P1 lesson; reschedule to the next suitable morning slot if unavailable.
- **Power-supply failure risk:** Check all six power supplies before students enter; if fewer than six are usable, defer and rebook rather than reduce student bench access below the required time.
- **Lost bench-time risk:** Pre-position goggles, printed instructions, and station materials, use fixed group roles, and maintain the 5-minute setup/40-minute bench/5-minute pack-up structure.
- **Projector or device-access risk:** All instructions, observations, checkpoints, and answer prompts are available in printed form rather than relying on mobile devices.
