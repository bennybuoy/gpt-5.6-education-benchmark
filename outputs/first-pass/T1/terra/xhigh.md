## Practical placement justification

### Practical 1: Lesson 3, Week 1 Wednesday P1 — C3
- **Placement:** This practical occurs immediately after C1 and C2 and introduces C3 through direct construction and testing of a continuous path, source, and load.
- **Equipment and grouping:** The class works in six fixed bench groups of 5, 5, 5, 5, 4, and 4 students. Each group uses one power supply, using all six available supplies but not exceeding the limit. All 28 students use safety goggles from the class set of 30. No glassware, digital multimeters, mobile devices, or fume cupboard are required.
- **Usable bench time:** Four minutes are allocated for setup and briefing, 42 minutes for circuit construction, testing, and paper recording, and four minutes for pack-up. This provides more than 40 minutes of usable bench time.
- **Timetable suitability:** Wednesday P1 avoids the Friday P5 restriction and the lack of technician support after 1:00 pm.

### Practical 2: Lesson 8, Week 3 Wednesday P1 — C7
- **Placement:** This practical follows C6 and provides the required direct support for C7 before students use circuit models quantitatively in C8.
- **Equipment and grouping:** The same six bench groups each use one power supply and their station circuit arrangement, remaining within the six-power-supply limit. All students use one of the 30 goggles. Digital multimeters and glassware are not required, so their limits are not exceeded.
- **Usable bench time:** Four minutes are used for setup and role allocation, 42 minutes for constructing, comparing, and recording one-path and multiple-path arrangements, and four minutes for pack-up.
- **Timetable suitability:** This is the only practical in Week 3, so the plan remains below the maximum of two practical lessons per week and avoids afternoon technician limitations.

| Checkpoint | Lesson | Content checked | Same-lesson feedback |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Continuous-path check | 4 | C3: continuous path, source, and load | Students complete an 8-minute paper check, then use projected worked answers and a teacher-led correction process for 7 minutes; each student retains a marked response and a next-step correction. |

Both checkpoints are low-stakes and completed within 15 minutes.

## Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals if a practical is unavailable
- If **Lesson 3** cannot run, C4 and all later content must be deferred until the C3 practical is rescheduled, because C3 is required before C4. The C3 formative checkpoint moves with the rescheduled practical.
- If **Lesson 8** cannot run, C8, C9, and the final summative task must be deferred until the C7 practical is completed, because C7 is required before C8 and the summative task assesses C7–C9 together.

### Timetable conflicts
- No practical is scheduled on Friday P5.
- Both practicals are scheduled on Wednesday P1, avoiding the absence of dedicated technician support after 1:00 pm.
- Only one practical occurs in Week 1 and one in Week 3, meeting the maximum of two practicals per week.

### Risks and mitigation
1. **Power-supply failure:** The teacher tests all six power supplies before each practical; if one fails, the practical is rescheduled rather than running with fewer than six functional bench groups.
2. **Unsafe or incorrect circuit connections:** Students wear goggles, sources are de-energised before alterations, and the teacher checks each group’s first arrangement before testing.
3. **Loss of usable bench time during setup or pack-up:** Station materials are prepared before the lesson, roles are allocated quickly, and a visible 4–42–4 minute timing structure is used.
4. **Early dismissal disruption:** No practical or summative task is scheduled on Friday P5.
5. **Crowding at fixed benches:** Students remain in assigned groups of no more than five and use defined roles such as assembler, checker, recorder, and pack-up lead.
