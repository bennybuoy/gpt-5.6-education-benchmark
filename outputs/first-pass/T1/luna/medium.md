## 1. Lesson-by-lesson sequence

| Lesson | Scheduled day/period | Content statement addressed | Lesson type | Brief activity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Monday P2, Week 1 | C1–C2 | Theory | Students construct a simple model of a system and classify examples of energy transfer as heat, light, motion or electric current. |
| 2 | Tuesday P4, Week 1 | C3 | Practical | In six groups, students assemble and test circuits to identify the continuous path, source and load required for operation. |
| 3 | Wednesday P1, Week 1 | C3, C4 | Formative | Students complete a 15-minute C3 checkpoint, receive immediate feedback, and then develop a definition of current and its ampere measurement. |
| 4 | Thursday P3, Week 1 | C5–C6 | Theory | Teacher modelling and worked examples introduce voltage, volts, resistance, and the factors affecting resistance. |
| 5 | Friday P5, Week 1 | C6 | Formative | Students complete a 15-minute C6 checkpoint and use feedback to correct explanations about how material, length, thickness and temperature affect resistance. |
| 6 | Monday P2, Week 2 | C7 | Practical | Groups compare one-path and multiple-path circuit arrangements and record how the arrangements behave when a pathway is interrupted. |
| 7 | Tuesday P4, Week 2 | C8 | Theory | Students apply \(V = IR\) to simple resistive-circuit examples and interpret the relationship between voltage, current and resistance. |
| 8 | Wednesday P1, Week 2 | C9 | Theory | Students examine circuit models, identify their assumptions, and explain situations in which a model may fail. |
| 9 | Thursday P3, Week 2 | C7–C9 | Summative | In a task completed within 30 minutes, students compare series and parallel models, use \(V = IR\), and explain limitations of the models. |

## 2. Prerequisite chain

**C1 → C2 → C3 → C4 → C5 → C6 → C7 → C8 → C9**

## 3. Practical placement justification

### Practical 1: Lesson 2, Tuesday P4, Week 1
- It is placed immediately after C1–C2 so students can apply the model and energy-transfer ideas to the required circuit conditions in C3.
- It directly supports C3 by requiring students to identify a continuous path, source and load. It provides an observational basis for the later treatment of current in C4.
- Six bench groups are used, with no more than five students per group.
- One power supply is allocated to each group, using all six available power supplies. Four digital multimeters can be rotated between groups if current measurements are included; no group requires simultaneous access.
- The class set of 30 goggles covers all 28 students. Glassware and hand lenses are not required.
- Five minutes are reserved for setup, 40 minutes for bench work, and five minutes for pack-up, giving the required minimum of 40 usable minutes.

### Practical 2: Lesson 6, Monday P2, Week 2
- It is placed after C6, allowing students to use their understanding of resistance before comparing the one-path and multiple-path arrangements required by C7.
- It directly supports C7 and prepares students for the quantitative circuit modelling in C8.
- Six groups of up to five students are used. Each group has access to one power supply; the four digital multimeters are rotated through groups or used at shared measurement points.
- Goggles are available for all students; glassware and hand lenses are not required.
- Five minutes are allocated to setup, 40 minutes to constructing, comparing and recording circuit behaviour, and five minutes to pack-up.

Both practicals occur before 1:00 pm, avoid Friday P5, and ensure there are no more than two practical lessons in either week.

## 4. Formative cadence

1. **Lesson 3, Wednesday P1 — C3 checkpoint**
   - Students complete a short circuit-diagram and explanation task identifying the continuous path, source and load.
   - The teacher marks or checks responses during the lesson, gives whole-class correction using the projector, and provides individual verbal or written feedback before the lesson ends.

2. **Lesson 5, Friday P5 — C6 checkpoint**
   - Students complete a short explanation and application task about resistance and the effects of material, length, thickness and temperature.
   - The teacher displays model responses, discusses common errors, and returns annotated responses or verbal feedback within the same lesson.

Both checkpoints are designed for completion in 15 minutes and are low-stakes.

## 5. Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals if a practical is unavailable
- If Practical 1 is unavailable, C3 and C4 can still be taught through diagrams and teacher explanation, but direct practical application of circuit requirements and current measurement must be deferred until a practical opportunity is available.
- If Practical 2 is unavailable, C7 and C8 can be taught theoretically, but practical comparison of series and parallel arrangements and evidence-based application of the model must be deferred.
- C9 and the summative task should proceed only if students have received the planned conceptual teaching; any missing practical evidence should be recorded as deferred rather than assumed.

### Timetable conflicts
- No practical is scheduled on Friday P5.
- The practicals are scheduled Monday P2 and Tuesday P4, both within the listed L4 availability and before the absence of a dedicated technician after 1:00 pm.
- The sequence uses no more than two practical lessons per week and does not require the mobile-device trolley.

### Risks and mitigations
- **Equipment bottleneck:** Only four digital multimeters are available for six groups. **Mitigation:** rotate the meters or establish shared measurement points while other students complete observations and records.
- **Insufficient usable bench time:** Setup or pack-up could reduce the 40-minute minimum. **Mitigation:** prepare labelled equipment before the lesson, use a five-minute setup and five-minute pack-up routine, and assign group equipment roles.
- **Circuit construction errors or unsafe handling:** Students may connect circuits incorrectly. **Mitigation:** provide printed circuit steps, demonstrate the setup before bench work, require goggles, and inspect circuits before testing.
- **Mixed-ability differences:** Some groups may finish substantially earlier or later. **Mitigation:** provide core instructions plus an extension comparison question, and use structured group roles.
- **Early dismissal risk on Friday:** Practical work could be interrupted. **Mitigation:** schedule no practical on Friday P5.

## 6. Adjustments

### Practical lessons 2 and 6
- Provide the two students with printed, numbered instructions and labelled diagrams before practical work begins.
- Read the instructions aloud, model the first step, and check understanding privately before equipment is used.
- Provide extra processing time through a five-minute individual extension within the lesson while other groups begin recording or reviewing results.
- Assign a predictable role, such as equipment checker or recorder, while allowing participation in construction and observation.
- Use frequent teacher check-ins and accept clearly labelled diagrams or written observations as evidence of learning.
- Keep both students in a supervised group close to the teacher and provide a quiet location for completing the written record if required.

### Formative checkpoints in lessons 3 and 5
- Supply a printed copy with clear spacing, short sections and key terms presented separately.
- Read each section aloud and clarify task instructions without supplying answers.
- Design the checkpoint to take 15 minutes, with an additional five minutes available for the two students as an adjustment.
- Provide feedback in written form as well as verbally, using one strength and one correction for each response.
- Permit the students to complete the task in a lower-distraction seat and check that feedback has been understood before the lesson ends.
