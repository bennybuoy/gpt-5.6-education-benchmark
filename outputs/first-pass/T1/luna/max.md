## 3. Practical placement justification

**Practical 1: Lesson 4, Week 1 Thursday P3**

- It follows the teaching and formative check of C3, allowing students to apply the requirements for a source, load and continuous path while directly supporting C4.
- The 28 students use six groups: five groups of five and one group of three, with one fixed bench station per group.
- Six power supplies are allocated, one per group. Four digital multimeters are rotated so no more than four are in use at once and every group receives a measurement turn.
- Twenty-eight safety goggles are used, leaving two spares; no glassware or hand lenses are required, so the limits of eight glassware sets and twelve hand lenses are not approached. The fume cupboard is not required.
- Instructions and recording sheets are printed, so no mobile devices are needed.
- Timing is **5 minutes setup, 40 minutes usable bench work, and 5 minutes pack-up**. The teacher pre-checks the stations and students manage routine setup and pack-up, so a technician is not required.

**Practical 2: Lesson 8, Week 2 Wednesday P1**

- It follows C7 and precedes C9, so students can compare one-path and multiple-path circuits and develop the quantitative model in C8 before considering model limits.
- The same six groups use the six fixed stations and six power supplies; four digital multimeters are rotated through the groups for sequential voltage and current readings.
- The class set of 30 goggles covers all 28 students, while glassware, hand lenses and the fume cupboard are unused.
- Printed data tables support groups waiting for a multimeter and eliminate reliance on mobile devices.
- Timing is again **5 minutes setup, 40 minutes usable bench work, and 5 minutes pack-up**; the multimeter rotation occurs within the 40-minute bench block.
- There is one practical in each week, and neither practical is scheduled for Friday P5.

## 4. Formative cadence

- **Checkpoint 1 — Lesson 3, after C3:** A 15-minute, low-stakes paper task asks students to identify the source, load and continuous path. The teacher uses a short marking key, gives whole-class corrections, and returns annotated responses or individual feedback before the lesson ends.
- **Checkpoint 2 — Lesson 6, after C6:** A 15-minute, low-stakes paper task asks students to explain resistance and relate it to material, length, thickness and temperature. The teacher returns marked responses and discusses corrections within the same lesson.

## 5. Deferrals, timetable conflicts and risks

### Deferrals

- If Practical 1 is unavailable and no replacement lesson is provided, **C4** must be deferred; because the chain is sequential, C5–C9, the second checkpoint and the summative task must also move until C4 is taught. A replacement practical is required to retain the two-practical minimum.
- If Practical 2 is unavailable, **C8** is not taught as planned, so C9 and the final summative task must be deferred until C8 is covered. C7 and the second checkpoint can remain in place, but a replacement practical is still required.

### Timetable conflicts

No timetable conflict is identified: all lessons use listed L4 availability, the practicals are in Week 1 Thursday P3 and Week 2 Wednesday P1, no practical uses Friday P5, and the final is scheduled before the Friday early-dismissal risk. Teacher/student setup means neither practical depends on a technician after 1:00pm.

### Named risks and mitigation

- **Digital multimeter bottleneck:** Use four-meter rotations, with waiting groups completing printed construction, prediction and recording tasks.
- **Shared-room access or late handover:** Confirm the L4 booking, pre-check and pre-stage six stations, and reserve five minutes for pack-up.
- **Technician unavailability:** Use teacher-led pre-checks and student-managed setup; do not rely on technician assistance.
- **Friday attendance or early dismissal:** Schedule no practical or summative task on Friday P5; if the theory lesson is lost, defer the sequence rather than breaking the prerequisite chain.
- **Mixed ability and processing differences:** Use structured groups, defined roles, printed procedures and staged teacher instructions.

## 6. Adjustments

For both students with documented adjustments:

- **Practical 1, Lesson 4:** Provide printed, step-by-step instructions and a printed data table before the lesson; check the first step individually, give an early multimeter turn, and allow extra processing time during the pack-up window while the teacher or group secures the station.
- **Practical 2, Lesson 8:** Provide printed comparison instructions and a \(V=IR\) recording template; give staged prompts, an early measurement turn and extra time to complete the written record while pack-up occurs.
- **Formative 1, Lesson 3:** Provide printed checkpoint instructions and a response scaffold before the 15-minute task; allow extra processing time in the feedback window and give individual feedback before the lesson ends.
- **Formative 2, Lesson 6:** Provide the printed prompt and response scaffold in advance, allow extra processing time after the core 15-minute window if required, and return individual feedback within the same lesson.
