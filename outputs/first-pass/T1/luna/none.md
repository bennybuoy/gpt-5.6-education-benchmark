## 1. Lesson-by-lesson plan

| Lesson | Day/period | Content statement | Type | Activity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Monday P2 | C1 | Theory | Students analyse simple representations and develop a model explaining or predicting a system’s behaviour. |
| 2 | Tuesday P4 | C2 | Theory | Students classify examples of energy transfer as heat, light, motion, or electric current. |
| 3 | Wednesday P1 | C3 | Practical | In six groups, students construct circuits using a source, conducting path, and load, then identify open and closed paths. |

| 9 | Friday P5 | C9 | Theory | Students evaluate when a circuit model is useful and identify assumptions or conditions that may cause it to fail. |
| 10 | Monday P2 | C7–C9 | Summative | Students complete a timed task comparing series and parallel circuits, applying \(V = IR\), and explaining model limitations. |

## 2. Prerequisite chain

**C1 → C2 → C3 → C4 → C5 → C6 → C7 → C8 → C9**

Each statement is taught after the preceding statement because the sequence moves from modelling and energy transfer, to circuit requirements and measurement, then resistance, circuit arrangements, quantitative modelling, and model limitations.

## 3. Practical placement justification

### Practical 1: Lesson 3, Wednesday P1
This follows C1 and C2 and directly supports C3, with students constructing circuits and identifying a continuous path, source, and load. Wednesday P1 is suitable because it is not Friday P5 and allows practical work before the first formative checkpoint.

The class forms six groups of up to five students, matching the six fixed bench stations. Each group uses one circuit set; the plan stays within the limits of six power supplies and eight glassware sets. Digital multimeters are not required for this practical, so the four-meter limit is not exceeded. All students use the class set of safety goggles.

The teacher prepares equipment before the lesson; students use 5 minutes for entry and safety instructions, 40 minutes for construction and testing, and 5 minutes for pack-up, providing the required minimum 40 minutes of usable bench time.

### Practical 2: Lesson 7, Wednesday P1
This is placed after C6 so students can apply resistance ideas while directly addressing C7 and preparing for C8. Wednesday P1 avoids the Friday restriction and remains within the maximum of two practical lessons per week.

Students again work in six groups of up to five. Each group uses one power supply and shared circuit components, remaining within six power supplies and eight glassware sets; no more than four digital multimeters are needed because groups rotate access or use teacher-prepared readings. Goggles are available for all 28 students. No fume cupboard is required.

Setup is completed before class. The lesson allocates 5 minutes to instructions, 40 minutes to constructing and comparing circuits, and 5 minutes to pack-up, meeting the 40-minute bench-time requirement.

## 4. Formative cadence

- **Lesson 4 checkpoint:** Checks C3–C4 by asking students to identify circuit requirements, explain current as charge flow, and interpret current measurements. Students complete it in 15 minutes; the teacher marks or reviews responses immediately, gives whole-class corrections, and provides brief individual feedback before the lesson ends.
- **Lesson 6 checkpoint:** Checks C6 by asking students to predict how material, length, thickness, and temperature affect resistance. It takes 15 minutes; the teacher displays correct reasoning, discusses common errors, and gives written or verbal feedback in the same lesson.

## 5. Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals
- If Practical 1 is unavailable, practical evidence for C3 must be deferred; C3 can be taught through a teacher demonstration or circuit diagrams, but the construction investigation should occur before the Lesson 4 checkpoint where possible.
- If Practical 2 is unavailable, direct practical comparison of C7 must be deferred; C7 can be introduced using diagrams, while C8 can use supplied or teacher-generated data. The summative task remains scheduled but must use modelled data rather than student-generated practical data.
- C9 should not be assessed before C8 because it requires students to evaluate the quantitative model and its assumptions.

### Timetable conflicts
- No practical is scheduled on Friday P5.
- Each practical is scheduled for 50 minutes with 40 minutes usable bench time.
- No week contains more than two practical lessons.
- The plan uses only the listed available periods; Lesson 10 occurs on Monday P2 in the following timetable cycle if required.

### Risks and mitigations
1. **Equipment shortage or malfunction:** Pre-check power supplies, meters, and components; use group rotations and prepared data as a backup.
2. **Insufficient setup or pack-up time:** Prepare labelled equipment before class and assign student pack-up roles.
3. **Safety incident involving circuits or glassware:** Give printed safety instructions, require goggles, inspect setups, and stop work for unsafe arrangements.
4. **Uneven group participation:** Allocate rotating roles such as builder, recorder, tester, and reporter.
5. **Early dismissal on Friday:** Keep Friday P5 theory-only and schedule the summative task on Monday P2.

## 6. Adjustments

For both students, printed instructions, diagrams, vocabulary support, and clearly sequenced steps are provided in advance and at the start of each activity; extra processing time is allowed without reducing the learning target.

- **Practical 1:** Provide an illustrated circuit-construction checklist, a pre-labelled equipment layout, and teacher check-ins before testing; allow the students to observe a completed example and record responses using a structured table.
- **Practical 2:** Provide separate printed series and parallel circuit diagrams, a comparison table, and additional time for recording observations; allow a defined group role that supports participation without requiring rapid verbal responses.
- **Formative Lesson 4:** Provide a printed version with diagrams and enlarged response spaces, read or clarify instructions, and allow extra completion time within the lesson.
- **Formative Lesson 6:** Provide a printed factor-by-factor scaffold and sentence starters, permit extra processing time, and give feedback verbally and in writing before the lesson ends.
