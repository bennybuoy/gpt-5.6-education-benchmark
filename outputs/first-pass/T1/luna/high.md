## 1. Lesson sequence

| Lesson | Scheduled day/period | Content statement addressed | Type | Activity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Week 1 Monday P2 | C1 | Theory | Students analyse simple representations and define a model as a simplified representation used to explain or predict behaviour. |
| 2 | Week 1 Tuesday P4 | C2 | Theory | Students classify examples of energy transfer as heat, light, motion or electric current. |
| 3 | Week 1 Wednesday P1 | C3 | Practical | In six groups, students construct and inspect circuits to identify a continuous path, a source and a load. |
| 4 | Week 1 Thursday P3 | C3 | Formative | Students complete a 15-minute low-stakes checkpoint identifying whether circuit diagrams contain a continuous path, source and load, followed by same-lesson feedback. |
| 5 | Week 1 Friday P5 | C4 | Theory | Students use circuit examples to describe current as flow of charge and identify the ampere as its unit. |
| 6 | Week 2 Monday P2 | C5 | Theory | Students develop the meaning of voltage as energy transferred per unit charge and identify the volt as its unit. |
| 7 | Week 2 Tuesday P4 | C6 | Theory | Students compare how material, length, thickness and temperature affect resistance and how resistance opposes current. |
| 8 | Week 2 Wednesday P1 | C6 | Formative | Students complete a 15-minute checkpoint explaining resistance factors, followed by projected answers and teacher feedback in the same lesson. |
| 9 | Week 2 Thursday P3 | C7, C8 | Practical | Groups compare one-path and multiple-path circuits and use selected voltage/current readings to apply \(V=IR\) to simple resistive circuits. |
| 10 | Week 2 Friday P5 | C9; summative assessment of C7–C9 | Summative | After a brief explanation of model limits, students complete a 30-minute task comparing series and parallel models, using \(V=IR\), and identifying where assumptions fail. |

The sequence uses one practical in each week, with no practical on Friday P5.

## 2. Prerequisite chain

**C1 → C2 → C3 → C4 → C5 → C6 → C7 → C8 → C9**

## 3. Practical placement justification

### Practical 1: Lesson 3, Week 1 Wednesday P1

- It follows C1 and C2 and is placed before C4 so students first establish the required conditions for a circuit in C3.
- It directly supports C3 by requiring students to identify the continuous path, source and load.
- There are six groups: four groups of five and two groups of four, matching the six fixed bench stations.
- Each group uses one power supply, for six total; no digital multimeters, glassware sets or hand lenses are required.
- The class set of 30 goggles is sufficient for 28 students.
- Timing is **5 minutes setup, 40 minutes usable bench time, and 5 minutes pack-up**; the teacher pre-checks and places the circuit materials before students begin.

### Practical 2: Lesson 9, Week 2 Thursday P3

- It follows C6 and the C6 formative checkpoint, allowing students to apply resistance ideas to C7 and C8.
- It directly supports C7 through comparison of one-path and multiple-path circuits and C8 through \(V=IR\) calculations using simple resistive circuits.
- Each of the six groups uses one power supply, within the limit of six.
- Four digital multimeters are used in timed rotations between the six groups; groups without a meter construct, predict and record, then complete their measurements when a meter becomes available.
- No more than six groups are used, and no glassware sets or hand lenses are needed; 28 goggles remain within the class limit of 30.
- Timing is **5 minutes setup, 40 minutes usable bench time, and 5 minutes pack-up**. The teacher prepares and checks equipment in advance, so the practical does not depend on a technician after 1:00 pm or on the fume cupboard.

## 4. Formative cadence

- **Lesson 4, after C3:** A 15-minute low-stakes checkpoint checks recognition of a continuous path, source and load in circuit representations. The teacher displays correct responses, explains common errors and gives brief individual feedback before the lesson ends.
- **Lesson 8, after C6:** A 15-minute low-stakes checkpoint checks how material, length, thickness and temperature affect resistance. Annotated responses, projected answers and short teacher conferences provide feedback within the same lesson.

## 5. Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals

- If Practical 1 is unavailable, defer the C3 practical experience and reschedule it before claiming the required practical support for C3; C4 should follow explicit teaching and checking of C3.
- If Practical 2 is unavailable, defer the practical application of C7 and C8 and postpone the final task until students have completed the required C7–C8 learning; otherwise the two-practical minimum and the intended assessment preparation are not met.
- No practical can be moved to Friday P5 because of early-dismissal risk, and no week may contain more than two practicals.

### Risks and mitigations

1. **Digital multimeter shortage or failure:** Pre-check all four meters, use timed rotations, and provide a prepared recording structure so groups can continue constructing and predicting while waiting.
2. **No technician after 1:00 pm:** Pre-set and teacher-check all equipment, use no fume-cupboard procedure, and avoid any technician-dependent step.
3. **Early dismissal on Friday P5:** Do not schedule practical work then; if the summative task is interrupted, retain completed responses and move the unfinished task to the next available non-Friday period.
4. **Six-station congestion in a mixed-ability class:** Use fixed groups of 5, 5, 5, 5, 4 and 4 with assigned construction, observation, recording and checking roles.
5. **Extra processing time affecting completion:** Provide instructions and recording templates before practicals and checkpoints, and allow unfinished written explanations or checkpoint responses to be completed during the planned feedback time or the next lesson.

## 6. Adjustments

- **Lesson 3 practical:** The two students receive printed, step-by-step instructions before the lesson and at their bench, with key terms clearly identified; the teacher previews the sequence and allows extra time for recording the explanation after their practical procedure is complete.
- **Lesson 9 practical:** They receive a printed staged procedure, circuit-recording table and calculation layout in advance; the teacher checks each stage before progression, and they may use additional time for written observations and calculations after the bench rotation.
- **Lesson 4 formative checkpoint:** They receive the printed checkpoint and instructions in advance, with the teacher confirming the task sequence; they may use the feedback/reteach period to complete responses, while receiving same-lesson individual feedback.
- **Lesson 8 formative checkpoint:** They receive a printed question sheet and structured response space, with extra processing time available during the same lesson’s feedback block; the teacher returns marked or annotated feedback before the lesson ends.
