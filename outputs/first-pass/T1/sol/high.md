## 3. Practical placement justification

### Practical 1 — Lesson 4: Current and complete circuits

- **Placement:** It follows explicit teaching and formative checking of C3, so students already understand that a circuit needs a continuous path, source, and load before measuring current for C4.
- **Grouping:** The 28 students work as four groups of five and two groups of four across the six fixed bench stations.
- **Equipment:** Each group uses one of the six power supplies and a prepared source–load circuit; the four digital multimeters rotate between groups, with no more than four in use at once. All 28 students use goggles from the set of 30.
- **Rotation:** During each 20-minute phase, up to four groups measure current while the remaining groups construct, trace, interrupt, and restore continuous paths; groups then exchange tasks and meters.
- **Timing:** Equipment and printed instructions are pre-staged before the lesson. The period consists of 5 minutes for goggles and setup, 40 minutes of usable bench work, and 5 minutes for pack-up.
- **Staffing:** Kits are checked and staged before 1:00 pm, so the lesson does not depend on technician support during Thursday P3.

### Practical 2 — Lesson 8: Series/parallel paths and \(V=IR\)

- **Placement:** It occurs after C7 has established the distinction between one and multiple paths and after C4–C6 have introduced current, voltage, and resistance, allowing C8 to be investigated quantitatively.
- **Grouping:** Students remain in four groups of five and two groups of four at the six benches.
- **Equipment:** Each bench uses one power supply and a prepared simple resistive circuit. Four multimeters rotate between the six groups, with voltage and current measured sequentially using one meter per measuring group.
- **Rotation:** Four groups initially collect voltage and current data while two groups compare one-path and multiple-path circuit arrangements on paper and at the bench; access is then exchanged.
- **Timing:** Practical procedures are previewed in Lesson 7 and materials are pre-staged. Lesson 8 allows 5 minutes for setup, 40 minutes for measurement, calculations, and comparisons, and 5 minutes for pack-up.
- **Limits:** No glassware or hand lenses are required, and all recording and calculation are completed on paper rather than mobile devices.

## 5. Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals

- If **Practical 1** is unavailable, planned teaching of C4 is deferred and C5–C9 move accordingly, because the sequence uses the practical to establish C4 after C3.
- If **Practical 2** is unavailable, C8 is deferred; C9 and the summative task must also move because both depend on C8.
- A cancelled practical is rescheduled to the next non-Friday L4 period, while retaining no more than two practicals in that week.

### Timetable conflicts and risks

1. **Risk: Limited multimeter access.**  
   **Mitigation:** Use timed rotations, one meter per measuring group, and sequential voltage/current measurements; never exceed four multimeters in use.

2. **Risk: No technician after 1:00 pm.**  
   **Mitigation:** Check and pre-stage six labelled bench kits before 1:00 pm, provide printed setup diagrams, and avoid procedures requiring technician intervention during class.

3. **Risk: Shared-lab or equipment unavailability.**  
   **Mitigation:** Confirm L4 and equipment before each practical; if unavailable, run a printed consolidation lesson without advancing past the affected content statement and reschedule the practical.

4. **Risk: Friday early dismissal.**  
   **Mitigation:** No practical is scheduled on Friday; the Lesson 10 summative begins immediately and is limited to 30 minutes, with a later completion opportunity if dismissal interrupts it.

5. **Risk: Setup or pack-up reduces bench time below 40 minutes.**  
   **Mitigation:** Preview procedures in the preceding lesson, pre-stage equipment at all six benches, use labelled trays, and enforce the 5-minute setup/40-minute investigation/5-minute pack-up structure.

## 6. Adjustments

| Activity | Support for the two students with documented adjustments |
|---|---|
| **Practical 1** | Provide printed, numbered instructions and a circuit diagram in the preceding lesson; allow advance reading, assign a stable group role, check understanding individually before setup, and reduce repeated trials rather than rushing processing. |
| **Practical 2** | Provide a printed measurement sequence, \(V=IR\) calculation layout, and results table in advance; use clearly labelled steps, give an individual start check, and allow fewer repeated data sets while retaining voltage, current, resistance, and path comparisons. |
| **Formative Checkpoint 1** | Supply an uncluttered printed copy and allow the students to begin five minutes before the standard start, giving them up to 15 minutes before whole-class feedback; also provide the feedback diagram in print. |
| **Formative Checkpoint 2** | Supply a printed prediction grid with one factor per row and allow an early start for up to 15 minutes of processing time; provide the correction guide in print and briefly confirm feedback individually. |
