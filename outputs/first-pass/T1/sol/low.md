## Unit sequence: Electric Circuits and Models

### 1. Lesson-by-lesson plan

| Lesson | Scheduled day/period | Content statement(s) | Lesson type | Brief activity description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Week 1 Monday P2 | C1 | Theory | Students compare a simple circuit representation with the system it represents and identify how models simplify behaviour. |
| 2 | Week 1 Tuesday P4 | C2, then C3 | Theory + formative | Students trace energy transfers and identify the continuous path, source and load in circuit diagrams, followed by a 15-minute C3 checkpoint. |
| 3 | Week 1 Wednesday P1 | C4, applying C3 | Practical | In six bench groups, students construct and interrupt simple circuits to relate a continuous path to current as flow of charge measured in amperes. |
| 4 | Week 1 Thursday P3 | C5 | Theory | Students use diagrams and worked examples to explain voltage as energy transferred per unit charge and identify volts as its unit. |
| 5 | Week 1 Friday P5 | C6 | Theory + formative | Students examine how material, length, thickness and temperature affect resistance, then complete a 15-minute C6 checkpoint. |
| 6 | Week 2 Monday P2 | C7 | Theory | Students construct and annotate models showing that series circuits have one path while parallel circuits have multiple paths. |
| 7 | Week 2 Tuesday P4 | C8 | Theory | Students solve scaffolded simple-resistive-circuit problems using \(V=IR\) and prepare a results table for the next practical. |
| 8 | Week 2 Wednesday P1 | C7, C8 | Practical | Groups compare series and parallel arrangements and use measured or shared voltage/current data to test quantitative predictions using \(V=IR\). |
| 9 | Week 2 Thursday P3 | C9; assessment of C7, C8, C9 | Theory + summative | After identifying assumptions and limits of circuit models, students complete a 30-minute task integrating circuit paths, \(V=IR\), and model limitations. |

### 2. Prerequisite chain

```text
C1: Models are simplified representations
  ↓
C2: Energy transfers between systems
  ↓
C3: Circuits require a continuous path, source and load
  ↓
C4: Current is flow of charge, measured in amperes
  ↓
C5: Voltage is energy transferred per unit charge, measured in volts
  ↓
C6: Resistance opposes current and depends on specified factors
  ↓
C7: Series circuits have one path; parallel circuits have multiple paths
  ↓
C8: Simple resistive circuits can be modelled using V = IR
  ↓
C9: Models have limits and may fail when assumptions break down
```

### 3. Practical placement justification

#### Practical 1 — Lesson 3: Continuous paths and current

- **Placement:** It follows explicit teaching and formative checking of C3, so students already understand the required source–path–load structure before investigating C4. It directly supports both C3 and C4 without introducing later concepts prematurely.
- **Grouping and equipment:** The 28 students form six groups of four or five at the six fixed benches. Each group uses one of the six power supplies; the class set of 30 goggles provides one per student. The activity does not require glassware or a multimeter for every group.
- **Timing:** Teacher-prepared station trays and printed procedures are placed at benches before Wednesday P1. The lesson uses five minutes for entry, goggles and safety instructions; 40 minutes of uninterrupted construction, testing and recording; and five minutes for shutdown and pack-up.
- **Staffing:** Wednesday P1 avoids the lack of a dedicated technician after 1:00 pm and allows teacher preparation before the lesson.

#### Practical 2 — Lesson 8: Series, parallel and \(V=IR\)

- **Placement:** C7 and C8 have been explicitly taught in Lessons 6 and 7, so the practical consolidates rather than precedes the prerequisite knowledge. It gives students direct evidence for the different path models and an opportunity to compare \(V=IR\) predictions with circuit data before the summative task.
- **Grouping and equipment:** Students again work in six groups of four or five, with one power supply per group and one pair of goggles per student. Four groups initially use the four digital multimeters while two groups calculate predictions and analyse supplied readings; multimeters are exchanged at the midpoint so all groups complete measurements. No more than six power supplies or four multimeters are used simultaneously.
- **Timing:** Equipment and printed tables are pre-positioned. Five minutes are allocated to safety and role allocation, 40 minutes to two 20-minute investigation rotations, and five minutes to power-down and pack-up.
- **Staffing:** Wednesday P1 avoids Friday’s early-dismissal risk and the after-1:00-pm technician limitation.

### 4. Formative cadence

| Checkpoint | Lesson | Content checked | Same-lesson feedback |
|---|---|---|---|
| Circuit requirements checkpoint | Lesson 2 | C3: identifying a continuous path, source and load and diagnosing an incomplete circuit | Students complete a 10-minute diagram task and five-minute peer check using a projected answer key; the teacher immediately corrects common errors and marks students requiring support before Practical 1. |
| Resistance checkpoint | Lesson 5 | C6: resistance and the effects of material, length, thickness and temperature | Students complete a 10-minute four-part response, then use a colour-coded projected solution for five-minute self-correction while the teacher gives brief targeted verbal feedback. |

Both checkpoints are low-stakes and take no more than 15 minutes.

### 5. Deferrals and risks

#### Deferrals if practical access is unavailable

- If **Practical 1** cannot occur, the hands-on application of C3 and teaching/application of C4 through circuit behaviour must be moved to the next available non-Friday laboratory period before proceeding to C5.
- If **Practical 2** cannot occur, the practical application of C7 and C8 must be rescheduled before the summative task so the two-practical minimum and the required C7/C8 practical support are retained.
- The summative assessment is deferred if C9 has not been taught or if the second practical has not been completed.

#### Timetable conflicts

- Friday P5 cannot be used for practical work because of early-dismissal risk; Lesson 5 is therefore theory and formative only.
- Tuesday P4 and Thursday P3 may fall after 1:00 pm, when no dedicated technician is available, so no practical is scheduled in those periods.
- Both practicals are placed on Wednesday P1, one per week, keeping the sequence below the maximum of two practicals per week.

#### Named risks and mitigation

1. **Shared-laboratory access risk:** Another class or room change could displace a Wednesday practical.  
   **Mitigation:** Confirm L4 in advance and reserve the following Wednesday P1 as the make-up slot; defer dependent content or assessment if required.

2. **Multimeter shortage risk:** Four multimeters cannot serve six groups simultaneously.  
   **Mitigation:** Use two timed rotations, with two groups completing calculations and analysing shared readings while the other four measure.

3. **Equipment or circuit failure risk:** A power supply, connection or load may fail and reduce bench time.  
   **Mitigation:** Test stations beforehand, provide a teacher demonstration dataset, and combine an affected group temporarily with a neighbouring group without exceeding five where possible.

4. **Insufficient practical time risk:** Slow entry or pack-up could reduce usable bench time below 40 minutes.  
   **Mitigation:** Pre-stage equipment, assign group roles in advance, provide printed procedures, and use fixed five-minute setup and pack-up routines.

5. **Safety and crowding risk:** Twenty-eight students working at six fixed benches may crowd equipment.  
   **Mitigation:** Use groups of four or five with assigned operator, recorder, safety checker and equipment manager roles; require goggles and power-off checks before circuit changes.

### 6. Adjustments

#### Practical 1

- Provide both students with printed, numbered instructions, a labelled circuit diagram, a results table and safety steps before the lesson.
- Seat them at accessible benches with supportive peers and stable roles.
- Allow them to begin reading during the entry/safety phase and provide additional processing time before each circuit change.
- Check understanding individually before power is switched on and reduce repeated trials if needed without changing the C3/C4 learning goal.

#### Practical 2

- Provide printed series and parallel diagrams, a step-by-step multimeter sequence, a pre-formatted data table and a printed \(V=IR\) support triangle.
- Place the students in groups that measure during the second rotation, giving them the first rotation for processing instructions and completing predictions.
- Give advance warning of rotation changes and individual teacher checks before measurement.
- Accept fewer completed measurements where necessary, while retaining comparison of both circuit types and at least one \(V=IR\) calculation.

#### Formative checkpoint 1

- Supply a printed checkpoint with enlarged circuit diagrams and one instruction per line.
- Read directions quietly to each student, allow the full 15 minutes, and provide feedback using the same printed diagrams rather than relying only on the projection.

#### Formative checkpoint 2

- Supply printed questions with a separate word bank for material, length, thickness and temperature.
- Allow the full 15 minutes, avoid time-pressure prompts, and provide marked examples and brief individual verbal feedback during self-correction.
