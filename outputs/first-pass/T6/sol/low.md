## 2. Worksheet repairs

**Title**  
**Evidence for Chemical Change**

**Learning intention**  
Distinguish observations from inferences and use multiple observations to decide whether evidence supports formation of a new substance.

**Success criteria**  
1. Label statements as observation or inference.  
2. Use at least two relevant observations before claiming a chemical change.  
3. State when evidence is insufficient.

**Worked example**  
Fizzing tablet in water:  
- **Observations:** Bubbles are seen and the temperature decreases.  
- **Inference:** A gas may have formed.  
- **Decision:** These observations alone do not identify the gas or prove that a new substance formed. More evidence is needed.

**Group task**  
Build an **Evidence–Inference–Decision** tool. Include rules that require:  
- observations to be separated from inferences;  
- at least two relevant observations before claiming chemical change;  
- a statement that evidence is insufficient when appropriate.  

Test the tool against the **two provided cases**, then revise **one rule**.

**Individual check — 5 marks**  
For the case provided:  
1. State two observations. **(2 marks)**  
2. Distinguish an observation from an inference. **(1 mark)**  
3. Give an evidence-based decision. **(1 mark)**  
4. State one limitation or item of further evidence needed. **(1 mark)**

**Exit ticket**  
“One observation is ___; one inference is ___; my decision is ___ because ___; evidence still needed is ___.”

**Teacher note at foot**  
Mark the individual check as follows: two observations (2); observation/inference distinction (1); evidence-based decision (1); limitation or further evidence (1).
