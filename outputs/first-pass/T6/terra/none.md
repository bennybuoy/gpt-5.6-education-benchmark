## 2. Worksheet repairs

- **Title:**  
  **Evidence for Chemical Change**

- **Learning intention:**  
  **Distinguish observations from inferences and use multiple observations to decide whether evidence supports formation of a new substance.**

- **Success criteria:**  
  **1. Label statements as observation or inference.**  
  **2. Use at least two relevant observations before claiming a chemical change.**  
  **3. State when evidence is insufficient.**

- **Worked example:**  
  **Fizzing tablet in water**  
  **Observation:** bubbles and temperature decrease.  
  **Inference:** gas may have formed.  
  **Decision:** observations alone do not identify the gas or prove a new substance; more evidence is needed.

- **Group task:**  
  **In groups, build an Evidence–Inference–Decision tool. Include a rule that a decision about chemical change must use at least two relevant observations. Test your tool against two cases and revise one rule.**

- **Individual check (5 marks):**  
  **For the case provided:**  
  **1. State two observations. (2)**  
  **2. Identify one statement as an observation or an inference. (1)**  
  **3. Make an evidence-based decision about whether the evidence supports formation of a new substance. (1)**  
  **4. State one limitation or further evidence needed. (1)**

- **Exit:**  
  **One observation is ___; one inference is ___; my decision is ___ because ___; evidence still needed is ___.**

- **Teacher note at foot:**  
  **Assess whether students distinguish observations from inferences, use at least two relevant observations, and state when evidence is insufficient.**
