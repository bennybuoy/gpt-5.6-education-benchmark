## Unit plan: Year 8 Science (10 lessons)

| Lesson | Scheduled day/period | Content statement addressed | Lesson type | Brief activity description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Week 1 Monday P2 | C1 → C2 | Theory | Students define a model as a simplified representation, then identify heat, light, motion and electric current as energy-transfer forms. |
| 2 | Week 1 Tuesday P4 | C3 | Theory/formative | Students learn the continuous-path, source and load requirements of a circuit, then complete a 15-minute circuit-checkpoint. |
| 3 | Week 1 Wednesday P1 | C4 | Theory | Students trace charge flow through simple circuit representations and interpret current measurements in amperes. |
| 4 | Week 1 Thursday P3 | C5 | Theory | Students represent voltage as energy transferred per unit charge and identify volts as its unit. |
| 5 | Week 1 Friday P5 | C6 | Theory/formative | Students examine how material, length, thickness and temperature affect resistance, then complete a 15-minute resistance-checkpoint. |
| 6 | Week 2 Monday P2 | C3–C4 (revisit) | Practical | In groups, students build basic circuits, verify the continuous path/source/load requirements, and measure current in amperes. |
| 7 | Week 2 Tuesday P4 | C7 | Theory | Students compare circuit representations to distinguish one-path series circuits from multiple-path parallel circuits. |
| 8 | Week 2 Wednesday P1 | C7 → C8 | Practical | Students test series and parallel resistive circuits, record voltage and current data, and use \(V = IR\) for simple calculations. |
| 9 | Week 2 Thursday P3 | C9 | Theory | Students identify assumptions in circuit models and explain how a model can fail when its assumptions no longer hold. |
| 10 | Week 2 Friday P5 | C7, C8, C9 | Summative | Students complete a 30-minute individual task classifying circuit paths, applying \(V = IR\), and explaining a model limitation. |

## Practical placement justification

### Practical 1: Lesson 6, Week 2 Monday P2 — C3/C4
- **Placement:** This practical follows explicit teaching of C3 and C4, allowing students to apply the circuit requirements before measuring current; it is scheduled in an early-week, early-day slot and is the first of two practicals in Week 2.
- **Equipment and grouping:** The class works in six groups of four or five students at the six fixed benches; each group uses one of the six power supplies, while four digital multimeters rotate between groups for current readings. All 28 students receive safety goggles from the class set of 30.
- **Usable bench time:** Circuit materials, printed instructions and data tables are pre-positioned before class; minutes 0–5 are for setup and safety routines, minutes 5–45 provide 40 minutes of bench work, and minutes 45–50 are for pack-up.

### Practical 2: Lesson 8, Week 2 Wednesday P1 — C7/C8
- **Placement:** This practical immediately follows Lesson 7’s teaching of C7 and introduces quantitative modelling through C8 before students learn C9; Wednesday P1 avoids Friday P5 and does not create a third practical in the week.
- **Equipment and grouping:** The same six groups use the six power supplies at the six benches; four digital multimeters rotate so groups can collect voltage/current data while groups awaiting a meter configure paths, record observations, and complete calculations. All 28 students use the available safety goggles.
- **Usable bench time:** Materials are pre-positioned before the lesson; minutes 0–5 are for setup, minutes 5–45 are 40 minutes of circuit construction, measurement and \(V = IR\) work, and minutes 45–50 are for pack-up.

## Formative cadence

| Checkpoint | Lesson | Content checked | Same-lesson feedback |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Circuit requirements check | 2 | C3: continuous path, source and load | Students complete a three-item printed check in 15 minutes; an annotated answer key is projected, students mark their sheets, and the teacher provides an immediate next-step mark or verbal prompt before the bell. |
| Resistance factors check | 5 | C6: material, length, thickness and temperature | Students complete a four-item printed check in 15 minutes; the teacher projects worked responses, students annotate their sheets, and the teacher confirms errors and next steps during the final feedback period. |

## Deferrals and risks

### Deferrals
- If **Lesson 6** is unavailable, no new authoritative content must be deferred because C3 and C4 have already been taught; the practical application of C3/C4 must be rescheduled to the next suitable Monday P2 or Wednesday P1 so the unit still contains two practicals.
- If **Lesson 8** is unavailable, **C8 must be deferred**, followed by C9 and the final summative task, because C8 is introduced through that practical and C9 depends on it.
- The Lesson 10 summative task must move if Lesson 8 cannot be rescheduled before it.

### Timetable conflicts
- No practical is scheduled on Friday P5; Lesson 10 is a non-practical 30-minute written summative task.
- The two practicals occur in Week 2 only, meeting the maximum of two practical lessons per week.
- Monday P2 and Wednesday P1 are used for practicals so preparation does not rely on a technician after 1:00pm; Tuesday P4 and Thursday P3 remain non-practical lessons.

### Named risks and mitigation
- **Digital multimeter bottleneck:** Only four meters are available for six groups; use a timed rotation while non-meter groups build circuits, check paths, record observations, or complete calculations.
- **Setup/pack-up overrun:** Pre-position materials and printed sheets, use fixed group roles, and enforce the 5-minute setup/5-minute pack-up routine.
- **Friday early-dismissal risk:** Start the 30-minute summative task immediately in Lesson 10 and retain printed copies for later completion if dismissal interrupts it.
- **Power-supply or circuit fault risk:** Teacher checks connections before activation and keeps groups at their assigned bench stations.
- **Safety-goggle availability:** Issue one goggle pair to each of the 28 students, remaining within the class set of 30.
