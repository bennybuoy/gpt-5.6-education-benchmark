# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture  
**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Cohort:** 120 students in four classes  
**Total:** 30 marks

## 1. Criterion and performance-band table

For 4-mark criteria: Emerging 0–1, Developing 2, Proficient 3, Exemplary 4.  
For 6-mark criteria: Emerging 0–1, Developing 2–3, Proficient 4–5, Exemplary 6.  
A zero indicates no assessable evidence.

| Criterion | Marks | Component | Emerging | Developing | Proficient | Exemplary |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| **G1. Investigation design** | 4 | Group practical | **Unworkable design:** Aim, variables, controls or safe method are absent, unclear or substantially unsuitable. | **Partly workable design:** Gives a basic aim and method but identifies or controls variables inconsistently. | **Controlled design:** States a clear aim, identifies variables and controls, and provides a safe, repeatable method. | **Rigorous design:** Justifies controls, range and repeats and gives a precise, safe method capable of producing dependable data. |
| **G2. Results and data processing** | 4 | Group practical | **Unreliable record:** Results are sparse, disorganised or processed incorrectly. | **Basic record:** Records some relevant results and performs limited processing; units, repeats or presentation may be inconsistent. | **Accurate processing:** Presents results clearly with appropriate units and accurately processes or graphs data to show patterns. | **Precise evidence set:** Organises repeated results accurately, selects effective processing and identifies anomalies or uncertainty. |
| **G3. Conclusion, evaluation and report communication** | 4 | Group practical | **Unsupported report:** Conclusion is missing or not connected to results; evaluation and communication are unclear. | **Simple report:** Gives a basic conclusion and identifies a general limitation or improvement. | **Evidence-based report:** Uses results to support a coherent conclusion and explains relevant limitations and improvements. | **Integrated report:** Constructs a concise, well-supported conclusion and justifies targeted improvements through critical evaluation. |
| **A1. Individual analysis of data** | 6 | Individual analysis | **Minimal analysis:** Data handling is absent, largely incorrect or does not identify a meaningful pattern. | **Basic analysis:** Completes some calculations or representations and identifies an evident pattern with partial accuracy. | **Accurate analysis:** Correctly processes data, identifies relationships and supports interpretations with specific values. | **Discerning analysis:** Compares patterns precisely and evaluates anomalies, variation or uncertainty using detailed evidence. |
| **A2. Individual explanation and evaluation** | 6 | Individual analysis | **Unsupported explanation:** Makes assertions about circuits or energy transfer with little connection to the authorised data. | **Partial explanation:** Provides a simple explanation with limited links between circuit behaviour, energy transfer and data. | **Coherent explanation:** Uses the data to explain energy transfer and circuit behaviour and evaluates the strength of the conclusion. | **Integrated explanation:** Develops a precise data-supported explanation and critically considers limitations or alternative interpretations. |
| **R1. Individual reflection** | 6 | Individual reflection | **Generic reflection:** Gives little verifiable detail about learning, decisions or personal responsibility. | **Descriptive reflection:** Describes the student’s role, learning and at least one challenge or improvement. | **Evidence-based reflection:** Uses specific examples to evaluate responsibility, learning, challenges and useful improvements. | **Critical reflection:** Insightfully distinguishes individual and group evidence and proposes specific, achievable strategies for future work. |

The group report is marked once. The same G1–G3 marks apply to verified group members; the contribution statement does not divide the marks.

## 3. Checkpoint schedule

| Date | Submission | Checked by | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1, Day 5** | Group practical plan; each student’s individual analysis outline | Class teacher; group membership and files logged | Missing group plan: G1 capped at **3/4** for the group. Missing individual outline: that student’s A1 capped at **5/6**. |
| **Week 2, Day 5** | Group draft results; each student’s individual analysis draft | Class teacher, with brief written or recorded feedback | Missing group results draft: G2 capped at **3/4**. Missing individual draft: that student’s A2 capped at **5/6**. |
| **Week 3, Day 5** | Full report, signed contribution statement, individual analysis and reflection | Class teacher; authenticity check completed before marking | Late or missing items receive marks only for assessable, authenticated evidence. Blocking rules apply to the analysis. |

A documented absence may receive an authorised revised checkpoint date. Failure to meet that revised date is treated as a missed checkpoint.

## 4. Absence pathways

| Circumstance | Required pathway |
|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | The student must not claim data collection. The teacher supplies and authorises secondary data for the student’s analysis and reflection. Any discussion of findings in those individual tasks must use that dataset, not the group’s data. The student may remain on the shared report if a non-collection contribution is documented and verified. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | Continue the standard portfolio with revised checkpoint dates where authorised. If the practical was missed, follow the practical-absence pathway; otherwise use personally collected data or teacher-authorised secondary data as necessary. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | A supervised completion plan is established. Individual work uses only personally collected or teacher-authorised secondary data. Group membership is recognised only where a report contribution can be verified. |
| **Absent for more than 2 weeks** | Offer, in writing, an **individual-only 30-mark alternative** using supplied secondary data. The 12 report marks become an individual secondary-data investigation report assessed through equivalent G1–G3 criteria; A1–A2 remain 12 marks and R1 remains 6 marks. Individual checkpoint dates are set. |

## 5. Authenticity protocol

### Contribution statements
- Every group report includes a statement signed by all three members identifying responsibility for planning, data collection, processing, writing and editing.
- The teacher checks it against checkpoint files, document history, practical observations and brief oral questions.
- Conflicting claims are investigated with all members. Group marks are not released until membership and contributions are verified.

### Authorised data
- Each supplied dataset receives a teacher-issued identifier.
- Authorisation records the student, reason for use and permitted tasks.
- Students attach or cite the identified dataset. Group data are authorised for individual use only where participation in its collection is verified; a student absent from the practical uses supplied secondary data instead.

### Plagiarism
- Detection uses comparison with drafts, document histories, other submissions and short oral verification of reasoning.
- The student is given an opportunity to identify and explain their original work.
- Only authenticated original evidence is marked. A plagiarised individual analysis activates the overall blocking decision.

## 6. Moderation procedure

- **Sample:** At least **24 of 120 portfolios (20%)**, including portfolios from all four classes, varied performance levels, absence pathways and any alternative tasks.
- **Independent marking:** The same sampled portfolios are marked independently by two teachers using the published criteria and caps.
- **Trigger:** A discrepancy **greater than 3 marks on any criterion** triggers remoderation.
- **Remoderation:** Marks are not averaged automatically. Teachers re-examine the evidence against the descriptor, calibrate using agreed examples and independently remark the affected criterion. Related portfolios are reviewed for consistent application.
- **Final decision:** The appointed moderation lead determines the final mark from the authenticated evidence and descriptors, records the reason, and confirms all blocking decisions.

## 7. Blocking decisions

A portfolio cannot receive Proficient or Exemplary overall when the individual analysis is:

1. **Missing** — there is no individual evidence of analytical understanding.
2. **Plagiarised** — authorship and individual achievement cannot be authenticated.
3. **Based on data neither personally collected nor authorised** — the evidence has invalid provenance and breaches the required data-use pathway.

These blocks apply regardless of the numerical total; the highest available overall band is Developing. Missing drafts cause only the published criterion caps and are not additional overall blocks.
