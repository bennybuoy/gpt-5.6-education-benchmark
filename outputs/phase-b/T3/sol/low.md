# Year 8 Depth-Study Portfolio Architecture

**Topic:** Energy transfer in simple electric circuits  
**Duration:** 3 school weeks  
**Cohort:** 120 students in 4 classes  
**Portfolio total:** 30 marks  
**Working arrangement:** Groups of 3 complete the practical report; analysis and reflection are individual.

## 1. Criteria and performance bands

| Criterion | Component | Marks | Emerging | Developing | Proficient | Exemplary |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| **G1. Practical plan and method** | Group practical | 4 | **0–1:** Plan is incomplete or unsafe; variables, equipment or steps are unclear. | **2:** Provides a workable plan with some identification of variables, equipment, safety and measurement steps. | **3:** Provides a clear, safe and repeatable method with appropriate variables, equipment and measurements. | **4:** Provides a precise, safe and highly repeatable method; variables and controls are justified. |
| **G2. Results and data presentation** | Group practical | 4 | **0–1:** Results are substantially incomplete, unclear or unsupported by recorded measurements. | **2:** Records relevant data, but tables, units, observations or graphs contain omissions. | **3:** Presents sufficient results accurately using appropriate tables, units and/or graphs. | **4:** Presents complete, accurate and logically organised results, including clear patterns and anomalies. |
| **G3. Group interpretation and evaluation** | Group practical | 4 | **0–1:** Makes unsupported statements about results or gives little evaluation of the practical. | **2:** Identifies a basic pattern and at least one limitation or improvement. | **3:** Explains the main pattern using evidence and evaluates limitations with realistic improvements. | **4:** Develops a well-supported interpretation and evaluates reliability, limitations and improvements in detail. |
| **A1. Individual data analysis** | Individual analysis | 6 | **0–1:** Uses little valid data or makes unsupported/inaccurate observations. | **2–3:** Identifies basic patterns and uses some authorised evidence, with errors or limited support. | **4–5:** Accurately analyses authorised data, identifies patterns or anomalies, and supports conclusions with evidence. | **6:** Provides a precise, systematic analysis that integrates patterns, comparisons, anomalies and quantitative evidence. |
| **A2. Individual explanation and reasoning** | Individual analysis | 6 | **0–1:** Explanation is absent, copied, or shows little connection between energy transfer, circuits and evidence. | **2–3:** Gives a partial explanation with some connection to the data. | **4–5:** Gives a coherent explanation of energy transfer in the circuit, supported by analysed evidence and justified reasoning. | **6:** Gives a comprehensive, logically developed explanation that evaluates evidence and addresses limitations or alternative interpretations. |

Individual reflection** | Individual reflection | 6 | **0–1:** Provides little specific reflection or cannot identify personal contribution and learning. | **2–3:** Describes personal contribution, learning and one challenge or improvement, with limited detail. | **4–5:** Evaluates personal contribution and learning using specific examples and proposes realistic improvements. | **6:** Provides an insightful, evidence-based evaluation of contribution, learning, collaboration and future improvement. |

**Overall reporting bands:** Emerging 0–11; Developing 12–19; Proficient 20–25; Exemplary 26–30, subject to the blocking decisions below.

## 3. Checkpoint schedule

| Time | Submission | Checked by | Consequence if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1, Day 5** | Group practical plan; individual analysis outline | Class teacher; group plan checked against contribution allocation and safety expectations | **G1 cannot receive 4/4.** If the individual outline is missing, **A2 cannot receive 6/6.** Work must still be submitted at the next opportunity. |
| **Week 2, Day 5** | Group draft results; individual analysis draft | Class teacher, with dated receipt recorded for each group and student | Missing group draft results means **G2 cannot receive 4/4** and **G3 cannot receive 4/4**. Missing individual analysis draft means **A1 and A2 cannot receive 6/6**. |
| **Week 3, Day 5** | Full portfolio: shared practical report and signed contribution statement; individual analysis; individual reflection | Class teacher, followed by moderation sample | Unsubmitted work receives no marks until valid evidence is provided. A missing individual analysis activates the overall blocking decision. |

A checkpoint cap removes only the possibility of full marks in the corresponding criterion; the final evidence is otherwise marked against the published descriptors. Documented absence is managed through the absence pathways rather than treated automatically as non-compliance.

## 4. Absence pathways

| Absence circumstance | Evidence pathway | Checkpoint and marking arrangements |
|---|---|---|
| **Absent for the practical session** | Student must not claim or analyse group data they did not collect. Their individual analysis and reflection use a teacher-supplied secondary dataset. They may receive group-report credit only for a verified non-data-collection section they actually complete. | Teacher records the absence and authorises the dataset in writing. Relevant checkpoint material is completed using that dataset. |
| **Absent for 1 week** | Student resumes the normal portfolio where authentic participation is possible. If the absence includes the practical, the practical-session pathway above applies. | Teacher sets a reasonable replacement checkpoint. The full-mark checkpoint cap is not applied where the replacement checkpoint is met. |
| **Absent for 2 weeks** | Teacher identifies which evidence can still be completed authentically. Individual analysis and reflection use authorised secondary data where personal collection cannot be verified. Group marks apply only to verified contributions. | A documented catch-up plan sets revised draft checks before final marking. Unverified group work cannot be credited to the student. |
| **Absent for more than 2 of the 3 weeks** | Student is offered an **individual-only alternative task worth 30 marks**, using supplied secondary data. It contains an individual practical-design/data-report section worth 12, analysis worth 12, and reflection worth 6. | Teacher sets equivalent outline, draft and final checkpoints. No group contribution statement is required; dataset authorisation is recorded. The same criterion mark limits and band standards apply in individual form. |

## 5. Authenticity protocol

### Contribution statements
- Every group report includes a table naming each section and the student or students responsible.
- All three members sign the statement before final submission.
- The teacher compares the statement with checkpoint drafts, observed practical participation and document/version records.
- Teachers may conduct brief individual verification questions. Unsupported or disputed contributions are not credited to that student until resolved.

### Secondary-data authorisation
- Only a teacher-issued dataset may replace personally collected data.
- Authorisation records the student, reason, dataset identifier and date.
- The student labels the dataset as supplied secondary data in both analysis and reflection.
- Data collected by an absent student’s group are not automatically authorised for that student.

### Plagiarism
- Teachers compare final work with drafts, group members’ submissions and other portfolio responses, and may ask students to explain their reasoning orally.
- Suspected copying is reviewed with the student and relevant records.
- Material not demonstrated as the student’s own is excluded from marking. The student may provide valid replacement evidence under teacher supervision where directed.
- A plagiarised individual analysis remains subject to the overall blocking rule unless valid, authentic replacement evidence is accepted.

---

## 6. Moderation procedure

1. **Sample:** At least **20% of portfolios**—a minimum of **24 of 120**—is selected across all four classes and across performance levels.
2. **Independent marking:** Two teachers independently mark every criterion in each sampled portfolio before discussing scores.
3. **Trigger:** A difference of **more than 3 marks on any criterion** triggers remoderation.
4. **Remoderation:** The teachers re-read the criterion descriptor, compare evidence and record reasons for their scores. A third teacher then independently reviews the disputed criterion.
5. **Flow-on check:** If a discrepancy indicates inconsistent interpretation, further portfolios from the relevant class or performance range are sampled and affected marking is adjusted consistently.
6. **Final decision:** The designated moderation lead makes the final recorded decision after considering both original marks and the third review. The moderation record includes changed marks and reasons.

---

## 7. Blocking decisions

A portfolio cannot receive an overall **Proficient** or **Exemplary** classification, regardless of its numerical total, if:

1. **The individual analysis task is missing** — there is no required individual evidence of analysis.
2. **The individual analysis is plagiarised** — the evidence does not validly demonstrate the student’s own achievement.
3. **The individual analysis uses group data the student did not personally collect and was not authorised to use** — the evidence lacks an authentic or approved data basis.

In these cases, the highest available overall classification is **Developing** until valid individual analysis evidence is accepted. Missing checkpoint drafts restrict full marks in the corresponding criteria but are not, by themselves, additional overall band blockers.
